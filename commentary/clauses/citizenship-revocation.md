# Clause Note: Citizenship Revocation And Due Process

## Why This Clause Exists

Citizenship loss is one of the most severe forms of public power over a person’s legal and political status.

If government can revoke citizenship through thin procedure or delayed review, equal membership becomes contingent and insecure.

## Why The Draft Uses An Explicit Process Floor

This draft requires constitutional minimums before revocation can take effect, including:

- notice
- an independent tribunal
- the right to counsel
- judicial review

That is not procedural excess. It is a recognition that citizenship is too foundational to leave wholly to future statutory discretion.

## Why This Matters Beyond Individual Cases

Without strong process, citizenship rules can become tools for exclusionary politics or selective intimidation.

The clause exists to make clear that loss of membership in the constitutional community cannot happen casually or politically.
