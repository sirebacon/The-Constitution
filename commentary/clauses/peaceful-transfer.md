# Clause Note: Peaceful Transfer Of Power

## Why Put Peaceful Transfer In Constitutional Text?

Because a democratic system is not secured merely by holding elections.

It is secured when those who lose can be lawfully removed from power and those who win can actually take office. If transfer depends only on restraint, custom, or goodwill, then the constitutional order is more fragile than it appears.

## Why This Draft Treats It As A Core Commitment

The peaceful transfer of power is one of the clearest dividing lines between constitutional government and disguised regime capture.

That is why the draft protects it through:

- anti-subversion rules
- election and certification safeguards
- transition protections
- an unamendable democratic core

## Why This Is More Than Ceremony

Transfer of power is not a symbolic rite. It is the moment when the state proves whether law outranks the incumbent.

This clause exists so that refusal to yield power is not treated as only bad behavior. It is treated as a constitutional attack on democracy itself.
