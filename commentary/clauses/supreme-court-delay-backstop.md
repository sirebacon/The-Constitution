# Clause Note: Supreme Court Delay Backstop

## What Problem This Solves

In expedited constitutional disputes, the Supreme Court can shape outcomes not only by deciding, but by waiting.

If the Court can freeze a case indefinitely, then nondecision becomes a hidden form of constitutional power.

## Why The Draft Uses Continuity And Finality

The solution here is not to micromanage every case on the Court's docket.

The draft instead uses a narrow anti-delay architecture for expedited constitutional matters:

- missed-deadline notice duties
- conduct review
- continuity of the lower-court judgment
- a later finality backstop for the parties if the Court still does not decide

## Why This Is Limited To Expedited Constitutional Cases

The purpose is to protect elections, emergency disputes, and similar time-sensitive constitutional questions.

A general deadline regime for all Supreme Court cases would be too rigid. This clause is justified because some constitutional disputes lose meaning if the system allows strategic silence.
