# Clause Note: The Unamendable Core

## Why Put Anything Beyond Amendment?

A democratic constitution should be open to revision, but it should not be legally open to self-destruction.

That is the basic reason for the unamendable core. Some provisions are protected not because they are every good value the draft contains, but because they are the minimum conditions that make democratic amendment itself legitimate.

## Why This Draft Uses A Narrow Core

The core is intentionally not a complete ideological program.

It protects things like:

- universal suffrage
- free and regular elections
- binding election results
- peaceful transfer of power
- equal citizenship
- judicial independence
- separation of powers

Those are not simply preferred policies. They are the conditions without which constitutional politics stops being genuinely democratic.

## Why The Clause Also Blocks Evasion

Modern anti-democratic projects do not always repeal democracy openly.

They often preserve language while destroying substance. That is why the draft blocks not only literal repeal of the protected core, but also amendments that functionally destroy democratic alternation in power or concentrate unchecked authority.
