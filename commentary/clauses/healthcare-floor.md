# Clause Note: The Healthcare Floor

## Why Put Healthcare In The Constitution At All?

Because formal liberty means less when access to basic and emergency care can be denied simply because a person cannot pay.

This clause treats healthcare access as part of the minimum material conditions of equal citizenship and human dignity.

## Why The Draft Uses A Floor Instead Of One Fixed Model

The Constitution should define what government must secure, not lock the country into one permanent administrative scheme.

That is why the draft protects:

- access to basic healthcare
- emergency treatment
- protection against inability to pay becoming a barrier to medically necessary care

without constitutionalizing a full healthcare code.

## Why This Design Is Stronger

It lets the constitutional system require real coverage while still leaving room for democratic argument over how the program is structured.
