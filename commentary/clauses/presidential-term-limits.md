# Clause Note: Presidential Term Limits

## Why Keep Term Limits?

In a presidential system, term limits are a structural guard against personal rule.

They reduce the risk that the office will harden into leader-centered politics where the constitutional order becomes identified with one person.

## Why This Matters More In Presidentialism

Parliamentary systems usually have more ordinary political ways to replace failed leaders.

Presidential systems have fixed terms, stronger personal mandates, and higher symbolic concentration in one office. That makes term limits more defensible as a constitutional safeguard.

## Why The Draft Also Includes An Anti-Evasion Rule

Without an anti-evasion rule, term limits can be undermined through succession games or vice-presidential rotation.

The draft therefore does not treat term limits as only a slogan. It tries to make them real enough to stop obvious workaround strategies.
