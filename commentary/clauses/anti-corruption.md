# Clause Note: Anti-Corruption And Anti-Capture

## Why Corruption Is Treated As Constitutional

Corruption is not just bad administration.

It changes how power is allocated, who public institutions serve, and whether citizens can trust that law is being applied for public rather than private ends. At scale, corruption and capture become constitutional threats.

## What This Clause Is Aimed At

The draft is designed against:

- self-dealing
- hidden financial dependence
- contractor capture
- regulatory capture
- office used for private enrichment
- informal immunity for powerful financial or political actors

## Why This Sits Across Multiple Articles

No single clause can solve corruption.

That is why the draft uses a layered structure:

- Article VI for democratic integrity and anti-subversion
- Article VIII for substantive ethics and anti-capture duties
- Article XIII for structural administrative integrity

The purpose is to make corruption harder to normalize as merely bad behavior inside otherwise healthy institutions.
