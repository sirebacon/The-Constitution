# Clause Note: Federalism And The Democratic Floor

## Why Federalism Is Not Treated As Absolute

Federalism can protect pluralism, experimentation, and local self-government.

But it can also become a shelter for democratic erosion, unequal citizenship, and rights denial if state autonomy is treated as a free-standing constitutional end.

## Why This Draft Uses A Democratic Floor

This draft preserves states as important constitutional actors, but only within a system that protects:

- republican and democratic government
- equal citizenship
- federal constitutional supremacy
- basic rights and lawful national standards

The point is to preserve federalism without allowing it to become a license for anti-democratic enclaves.

## Why Local Democracy Also Matters

The article does not only constrain the national government.

It also recognizes that states can overreach downward by using preemption or administrative power to block local democratic choices without a sufficient public interest. Federalism here is not romanticized as only a state-rights issue. It is treated as a layered constitutional order.
