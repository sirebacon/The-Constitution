# Why Commentary Is Separate

## The Basic Choice

This project uses two layers:

- constitutional text for binding rules
- commentary for explanation and justification

That split is deliberate.

## Why Not Put Justification In The Constitution?

Because constitutional text should do only a few things well:

- allocate authority
- impose duties
- set limits
- define remedies and backstops

Once drafting commentary is blended into the article text, the document starts reading like a hybrid of constitution, law review article, and implementation manual.

## What Separation Protects

Keeping commentary separate protects:

- citation stability
- readability
- translation clarity
- future maintenance
- a clear distinction between law and explanation

## What Commentary Still Adds

Separate commentary still lets the project explain controversial or unfamiliar choices, including:

- the unamendable core
- constitutional organs
- narrow emergency powers
- the decision to retain a safer presidential system instead of switching to parliamentarism
- anti-delay and anti-subversion backstops

That is the right balance for a public-facing constitutional project: clean law, visible reasoning.
