# Article XIV Notes

## What This Article Is Trying To Protect

Revenue power is one of the oldest tools of constitutional abuse.

Governments can use taxation and duties to fund the state fairly, but they can also use them:

- to reward allies
- to punish opponents
- to shift power from the legislature to the executive
- to create opaque or selective economic burdens

This article is designed to preserve legislative control over revenue while still allowing ordinary fiscal administration to function.

## Why Revenue Authority Stays With Congress

Taxation is not just administration. It is an exercise of coercive public power that should remain democratically accountable.

That is why this article keeps the basic revenue decision with Congress and sharply limits executive alteration of duties, imposts, and related burdens unless law clearly authorizes it.

## Why The Article Also Mentions Administrative Fairness

A constitutional tax system should not require citizens to navigate avoidable complexity just to comply with the law.

The draft therefore allows simplified and automatic administration while protecting due process and guarding against penalties caused mainly by administrative failure or unreasonable complexity.

## Why The Article Does Not Constitutionalize Tax Policy

The Constitution should set the democratic and legal floor for taxation.

It should not try to encode rates, brackets, filing software, audit practice, or the entire revenue code. That level of detail belongs in ordinary law.
