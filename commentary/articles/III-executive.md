# Article III Notes

## What This Article Is Trying To Fix

The present U.S. presidency combines broad political expectations with weak textual guardrails. Too much depends on tradition, self-restraint, and slow institutional reaction.

This article keeps a directly elected President, but narrows the conditions under which executive power can become personal rule.

## Why Keep A Presidency At All?

This draft does not treat presidentialism as the ideal democratic model in the abstract. It keeps the presidency because the project is trying to produce a replacement constitution that still feels recognizable and plausible in the United States.

The goal is not to preserve the current presidency as-is. The goal is to make a presidential system less abuse-prone and less dependent on unwritten norms.

## Why So Much Attention To Orders, Delay, And Removal?

Presidential systems fail when delay becomes a weapon and when impeachment becomes the only meaningful remedy.

That is why this article tries to:

- limit unilateral lawmaking through executive orders
- force clearer legal grounding
- support faster review for high-impact directives
- make succession and incapacity rules usable in real crises

## Why The Presidency Still Has An Age Floor

The draft lowers age barriers for office in general, but it does not eliminate them entirely for the presidency.

The reason is that constitutional age requirements should be modest and justified, not inherited uncritically. The presidency carries singular military, diplomatic, and emergency responsibilities, so the draft keeps a maturity floor for that office while rejecting the much broader exclusionary logic that often governs age rules elsewhere.

The basic judgment is:

- voters should do most of the filtering
- constitutional age bars should be used sparingly
- the presidency may still justify a higher threshold than legislative office because the office concentrates unique national power

So the draft's position is not that age never matters. It is that age floors should be limited to what is genuinely necessary and should not be inflated beyond that.

## What This Article Deliberately Does Not Do

It does not try to constitutionalize every detail of administration.

The article is meant to define authority, limits, and fallback structure. Operational details should stay in law unless they are necessary to keep the presidency from overwhelming the rest of the system.
