# Article XII Notes

## Why Constitutional Organs Exist

Some functions are too central to democratic legitimacy to leave entirely to ordinary partisan control.

This draft uses Constitutional Organs to protect election administration, accountability, and a few other system-preserving functions without placing them in the hands of the executive or ordinary agencies alone.

## Why This Is Not Just An Agency Article

These bodies are not meant to be a fourth branch with unlimited reach.

They exist because some tasks need:

- structural independence
- continuity through transition
- enforceable duties
- protection against internal or external sabotage

## Why Startup And Continuity Matter So Much

Constitutional organs are only as strong as their ability to begin functioning on time and continue functioning under boycott or obstruction.

That is why the draft pays attention to:

- vacancies
- temporary quorum
- bridge appointments
- continuity during transition
- anti-sabotage provisions for internal refusal to act

## The Main Tradeoff

The risk is overengineering.

That is why the article was later simplified. The architecture remains, but the text aims to state the constitutional floor rather than a full institutional code.
