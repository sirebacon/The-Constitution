# Article I Notes

## What This Article Is Trying To Fix

The current U.S. constitutional structure protects elections in principle, but leaves too much of the democratic system to partisan manipulation, under-specification, and institutional habit.

This article treats the electoral system itself as constitutional architecture rather than ordinary political spoil.

## Why So Much Electoral Design Appears In The Constitution

Election systems shape representation, party structure, legitimacy, and the ability of voters to replace those in power.

That is why this draft constitutionalizes:

- the right to vote
- automatic registration
- election timing
- core electoral methods
- party qualification and anti-cartel rules
- election administration independence

The point is not to freeze every technical rule forever. The point is to place the democratic floor above ordinary partisan rewriting.

## Why The Article Includes Anti-Cartel and Infrastructure Rules

Democracy can be distorted without formally abolishing elections.

That is why this article reaches not only direct ballot exclusion, but also exclusion from core public or publicly regulated election infrastructure. The aim is to stop procedural gatekeeping from becoming a substitute for open democratic competition.

## Why This Is Still Limited

The Constitution should not supervise every private debate host, civic association, or media platform.

The line here is deliberate:

- core public and publicly regulated election infrastructure belongs in the constitutional floor
- softer or more private coordination problems are mainly for statute, competition law, and election law

That keeps the article democracy-protective without turning it into a general code for political life.
