# Major Platform Denies Equal Candidate Access to Political Distribution Tools
Scenario: platform-denies-candidate-ad-tools.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A major digital platform denies a lawfully registered federal candidate equal access to political advertising and distribution tools that remain available to competitors. The scenario tests whether Article VI Section 7.5(c) gives the Electoral Commission a direct constitutional foothold to require nondiscriminatory access and audit the platform's political systems.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Electoral Commission must review the unequal platform access, order immediate nondiscriminatory access if a violation is found, and require an independent audit of the platform's political-content distribution tools by day 15. [Article VI Section 7.5 and Article XII]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - a major digital platform denies a lawfully registered federal candidate equal access to political advertising and distribution tools available to competing candidates. Article VI Section 7.5(c) requires major digital platforms to provide equal access to such tools for all lawfully registered political candidates and parties. [Article VI Section 7.5(c)]
- Day 9: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that Article VI Section 7.5 already provides a meaningful constitutional foothold against discriminatory denial of candidate platform tools, at least where the platform is providing structured political advertising or distribution services rather than merely ranking organic content. [Article VI Section 7.5(c)]
- Day 9: RESOLUTION - Electoral Commission found a violation of Article VI Section 7.5(c), ordered immediate equal access to the platform's political advertising and distribution tools, and opened an independent audit of the platform's political-content systems. [Article VI Section 7.5 and Article XII]
