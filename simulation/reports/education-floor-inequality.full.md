# State Leaves Students with Substantially Inferior Education
Scenario: education-floor-inequality.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A state's education funding structure leaves students in low-income districts with substantially inferior education on the basis of geographic location and income. Courts declare a violation under Art XVIII §2.2 and require a constitutionally sufficient remedy. Tests whether the education floor is judicially enforceable without courts prescribing specific policy solutions.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must require the state to adopt a lawful remedy within a defined period sufficient to restore the constitutional education floor for the affected students by day 91. [Article XVIII Section 2.2]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - State's education funding and resource allocation leaves students in low-income rural districts with substantially inferior education on the basis of geographic location and income, in violation of Article XVIII Section 2.2. [Article XVIII Section 2.2]
- Day 80: OUTCOME - Article XVIII Section 2.2 imposes a judicially enforceable floor: public education must be of sufficient quality to enable meaningful participation in civic and economic life, and no group may receive substantially inferior education on a prohibited basis. Courts determine whether the floor is met; the state chooses how to meet it. [Article XVIII Section 2.2]
- Day 80: RESOLUTION - Federal courts required the state to adopt a constitutionally sufficient remedy for the education floor violation within one year and retained jurisdiction to review adequacy. [Article XVIII Section 2.2]
