# Outgoing President Obstructs Electoral Certification and Transition
Scenario: electoral-certification-obstruction.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - Electoral Commission certifies the election result and the President-elect. The constitutional transition period begins. [Article III Section 13.1]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - President must cooperate fully with the President-elect's transition including security briefings, agency access, and office space by day 81. [Article III Section 13.1]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Electoral Commission certifies the election result and the President-elect. The outgoing President refuses to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition, denying security briefings and agency access, in violation of Article III Section 13 and the anti-subversion provisions of Article VI Section 1. The Accountability Commission opens an investigation. The transition proceeds under the constitutional mandate regardless of the outgoing President's obstruction.
- Day 5: BOTTLENECK - President refused to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition under Article III Section 13.1. [Article III Section 13.1]
- Day 5: VIOLATION - Outgoing President refuses to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition, denying the President-elect access to security briefings and agency records. [Article III Section 13.5 and Article VI Section 1]
- Day 8: EVENT - Accountability Commission opens an electoral subversion investigation into the outgoing President's transition obstruction. [Article VI Section 1 and Article III Section 15.7]
- Day 8: OBLIGATION - Accountability Commission must pursue the electoral subversion case through investigation and prosecution. [Article VI Section 1]
- Day 20: OUTCOME - The constitutional transfer of power proceeds. Presidential obstruction does not prevent the transition — it only creates additional criminal exposure for the outgoing President. [Article III Section 13 and Article VI Section 1]
- Day 20: RESOLUTION - Accountability Commission continued pursuing the electoral subversion case while the transition proceeded under constitutional mandate. [Article VI Section 1]
