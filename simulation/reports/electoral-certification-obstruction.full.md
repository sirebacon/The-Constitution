# Outgoing President Obstructs Electoral Certification and Transition
Scenario: electoral-certification-obstruction.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - Electoral Commission certifies the election result and the constitutional transition period begins. [Article III Section 13]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - President must cooperate with the constitutionally required transition by day 7. [Article III Section 13.1]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Electoral Commission certifies the election result and the President-elect. The outgoing President refuses to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition, denying security briefings and agency access, in violation of Article III Section 13 and the anti-subversion provisions of Article VI Section 1. The Accountability Commission opens an investigation. The transition proceeds under the constitutional mandate regardless of the outgoing President's obstruction.
- Day 5: BOTTLENECK - President refused to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition under Article III Section 13.1. [Article III Section 13.1]
- Day 5: OBLIGATION - Accountability Commission must open an electoral-subversion or transition-obstruction case by day 12. [Article VI Section 1 and Article III Section 13.2]
- Day 5: VIOLATION - Outgoing President refuses to cooperate with the constitutionally required transition, denying the President-elect access to security briefings and agency records. [Article III Section 13.1 and Article VI Section 1]
- Day 8: RESOLUTION - Accountability Commission opened an electoral-subversion and transition-obstruction investigation. [Article VI Section 1 and Article III Section 13.2]
- Day 20: OUTCOME - The constitutional transition proceeds despite presidential obstruction. Certification and term commencement are not dependent on outgoing presidential cooperation. [Article III Section 13]
