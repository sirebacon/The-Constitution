# Temporary Constitutional Organ Quorum Begins Operations
Scenario: constitutional-organ-bridge-startup.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - The Constitution is ratified and the first-cycle implementation period begins. [Article XIX Section 1 and Section 4]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Congress and appointing authorities must constitute the Constitutional Organs on the timeline required by the Constitution by day 365. [Article XIX Section 5.2]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Congress must provide by law for the first federal elections and orderly commencement of terms under the new constitutional order by day 365. [Article XIX Section 4.1 and Section 4.2]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Constitutional Organs are not constituted on time, the Supreme Court orders completion, the appointing actors still fail to act, and the Court then appoints the minimum temporary Commissioners needed for lawful quorum. The temporary quorum then begins startup and continuity functions before the ordinary appointment process is complete. The scenario tests whether Article XIX's bridge mechanism creates real operational continuity rather than a merely formal temporary appointment power.
