# Foreign Cyber Election Crisis
Scenario: foreign-cyber-election-crisis.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - A foreign adversary hacks voter systems, releases forged materials, and disrupts power near an election.
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Electoral Commission must protect the election through constitutional disclosure, transparency, and emergency administration powers by day 2. [Article I and Article VI]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must review any direct speech restriction imposed in response to the foreign attack by day 3. [Article V and Article XVII]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Weeks before a presidential election, a foreign adversary hacks voter systems, releases forged materials, and disrupts power near polling infrastructure. The President attempts direct federal control over state election administration and tries to suppress suspected foreign propaganda while also using non-military Article XVII countermeasures.
- Day 1: OUTCOME - The President uses Article XIV foreign-affairs tools short of war and without suppressing domestic political rights. [Article XVII foreign policy and national security tools]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Executive branch attempts to suppress suspected foreign propaganda directly rather than using disclosure and transparency tools. [Article V and Article XVII]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - President attempts direct federal control over state election administration outside the constitutional election framework. [Article I and Article X]
- Day 2: RESOLUTION - Electoral Commission used constitutional disclosure and emergency administration powers to protect election administration without direct federal takeover. [Article I and Article VI]
- Day 3: RESOLUTION - Federal courts blocked the direct speech restriction and required the government to use disclosure-based measures instead. [Article V and Article XVII]
