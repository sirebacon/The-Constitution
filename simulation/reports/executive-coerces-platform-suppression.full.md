# Executive Coercion of Platforms to Suppress Lawful Political Dissent
Scenario: executive-coerces-platform-suppression.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Senior executive officials pressure major digital platforms with threatened regulatory retaliation to suppress lawful political dissent without issuing a formal order. The scenario tests whether the Constitution treats coercive private suppression as state action and reaches it through Article V and Article XIII.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Accountability Commission must investigate whether executive officials used agency threats or regulatory leverage to induce viewpoint-based suppression by day 31. [Article XIII Section 2.0.5 and Article XII]
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must determine on expedited review whether the state-coerced suppression is attributable to government action and enjoin any unconstitutional pressure by day 11. [Article V Section 2.1 and Section 2.6; Article XIII Section 2.0.5]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Senior executive officials pressure major digital platforms through threatened regulatory retaliation to suppress lawful political dissent critical of the administration without issuing a formal order. The Constitution does not permit the executive to accomplish through coercive private pressure what Article V forbids it to do directly, and Article XIII Section 2.0.5 bars use of agency power for political retaliation. [Article V Section 2.1 and Section 2.6; Article XIII Section 2.0.5]
- Day 7: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that the Constitution can reach indirect censorship when private suppression is driven by government coercion rather than ordinary platform discretion. Article V protects against state action in substance, not only formal edicts. [Article V Section 2.1 and Section 2.6; Article XIII Section 2.0.5]
- Day 7: RESOLUTION - Federal courts held that the pressure campaign constituted state action through coercive use of threatened agency power, enjoined further retaliation, and ordered restoration of access where the suppression resulted from that coercion. [Article V Section 2.1 and Section 2.6; Article XIII Section 2.0.5]
- Day 22: RESOLUTION - Accountability Commission found that executive officials used threatened regulatory retaliation to induce viewpoint-based suppression and referred the responsible officials for prosecution or removal proceedings. [Article XIII Section 2.0.5 and Article XII]
