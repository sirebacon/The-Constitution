# Government Purchases Data to Circumvent Warrant Requirement
Scenario: data-purchase-warrant-circumvention.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A government agency purchases personal data from a private data broker — including location data, communications metadata, and association records — to obtain information it would need a warrant to collect directly. Courts apply the warrant requirement under Art V §10.3 and suppress the data.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must apply the warrant requirement to the purchased data under Article V Section 10.3 and suppress any evidence derived from it by day 15. [Article V Section 10.3 and Section 5.3]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Government agency purchases personal data from a private data broker — including location data, communications metadata, and association records — in order to obtain information it would need a warrant to collect directly, in violation of Article V Section 10.3. [Article V Section 10.3 and Section 5.3]
- Day 14: OUTCOME - Data purchased from a private entity to circumvent the warrant requirement is treated as government-obtained for all constitutional purposes under Article V Section 10.3. The warrant requirement cannot be evaded by routing collection through a commercial intermediary. [Article V Section 10.3 and Section 5.3]
- Day 14: RESOLUTION - Federal courts treated the purchased data as government-obtained under Article V Section 10.3 and suppressed it as collected without a warrant. [Article V Section 10.3 and Section 5.3]
