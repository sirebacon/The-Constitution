# Supreme Court Strategic Delay Before National Election
Scenario: supreme-court-strategic-delay.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - The Supreme Court hears a major constitutional case with obvious national political consequences. [Article IV Section 10.1]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Supreme Court must publish a public notice identifying the case and the reason for delay if no decision has issued within six months of argument by day 180. [Article IV Section 10.1]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Supreme Court hears a major election-administration case. Six months pass without a decision and without the required public notice explaining the delay. The delay is part of a wider pattern of politically timed nondecision-making by the Court, and the Judicial Conduct Board opens review. The Board later finds that the delay was strategic rather than attributable to genuine complexity or workload. The scenario tests whether the Constitution treats strategic judicial delay as misconduct without turning discipline into punishment for the substance of a ruling.
- Day 190: BOTTLENECK - Supreme Court failed to publish a public notice identifying the case and the reason for delay if no decision has issued within six months of argument by day 180 under Article IV Section 10.1. [Article IV Section 10.1]
- Day 190: OBLIGATION - Judicial Conduct Board must review the pattern of judicial delay and determine whether it reflects strategic misconduct rather than genuine complexity or workload. [Article IV Section 10.1 and Section 10.4]
- Day 190: VIOLATION - The Court appears to be using delay itself to affect the political timing of a major constitutional dispute. [Article IV Section 10.1]
- Day 210: EVENT - Judicial Conduct Board opens review of the Court's delay pattern and the apparent failure to provide the required public notice. [Article IV Section 10.1 and Section 10.4]
- Day 260: OUTCOME - The finding constitutes conduct-based grounds for judicial removal and does not rest on the substance of any judicial opinion. [Article IV Section 8.1, Section 8.2, and Section 10.1]
- Day 260: RESOLUTION - Judicial Conduct Board found that the delay pattern was strategic and intended to achieve political effect rather than attributable to genuine complexity or workload. [Article IV Section 10.1 and Section 10.4]
