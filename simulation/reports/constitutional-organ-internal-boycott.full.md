# Electoral Commission Internal Boycott of Required Certification
Scenario: constitutional-organ-internal-boycott.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A bloc of Electoral Commissioners refuses quorum or certification in order to block timely certification of a federal election. The scenario tests whether Article XII's anti-sabotage and continuity rules, together with expedited judicial relief, preserve the Commission's required constitutional function without creating a general judicial-management power over the Organ.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must issue expedited judicial relief sufficient to preserve the Electoral Commission's required certification function despite internal boycott by day 6. [Article XII Section 5.5 and Article I Section 10.4]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - A bloc of Electoral Commissioners refuses quorum or certification in order to prevent timely certification of a presidential election, threatening the Commission's constitutionally required function. [Article XII Section 2.7 and Section 5.5; Article I Section 10.4]
- Day 4: OBLIGATION - Electoral Commission must complete certification under the court-approved anti-boycott continuity procedures by day 9. [Article XII Section 2.7 and Section 5.5; Article I Section 10.4]
- Day 4: RESOLUTION - Federal courts ordered emergency anti-boycott relief, authorized temporary internal continuity procedures, and directed the Commission to complete certification on the constitutional timetable. [Article XII Section 5.5 and Article I Section 10.4]
- Day 7: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that Article XII Section 5.5 is operational: internal boycott does not disable a Constitutional Organ's time-sensitive constitutional function when expedited judicial relief and continuity procedures are available. [Article XII Section 2.7 and Section 5.5; Article I Section 10.4]
- Day 7: RESOLUTION - Electoral Commission completed certification through the court-approved continuity procedure despite the internal boycott. [Article XII Section 2.7 and Section 5.5; Article I Section 10.4]
