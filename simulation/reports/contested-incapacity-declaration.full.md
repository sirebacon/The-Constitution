# Contested Presidential Incapacity — Congress Misses Resolution Deadline

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - Vice President and Cabinet transmit a written declaration of presidential incapacity under Article III Section 11.2. The Vice President becomes Acting President. [Article III Section 11.2]
- Day 0: OUTCOME - The President may contest this determination within 4 days. If contested, Congress has 21 days from the contest to resolve the question by 2/3 vote. If Congress does not sustain the determination within 21 days, the President resumes the powers of office. [Article III Section 11.3]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Vice President and a majority of the Cabinet transmit a written declaration of presidential incapacity under Article III Section 11.2. The Vice President becomes Acting President. The President contests the determination within the required 4-day window under Section 11.3. Congress has 21 days to resolve the contest by a 2/3 vote. Congress fails to hold the required vote within 21 days. Under Section 11.3, when Congress does not sustain the incapacity determination within the required period, the President automatically resumes the powers of office. The scenario tests that the resolution deadline is enforced and that the constitutional default — presidential resumption of powers — applies without requiring further action.
- Day 2: EVENT - President transmits a written contest of the incapacity determination within the 4-day window. The 21-day congressional resolution period begins. [Article III Section 11.3]
- Day 2: OBLIGATION - Congress must resolve the contested incapacity determination by 2/3 vote of both chambers by day 23. [Article III Section 11.3]
- Day 25: BOTTLENECK - Congress failed to resolve the contested incapacity determination by 2/3 vote of both chambers by day 23 under Article III Section 11.3. [Article III Section 11.3]
- Day 25: OUTCOME - Under Article III Section 11.3, when Congress does not sustain the incapacity determination within 21 days, the President automatically resumes the powers of office. No further action by any branch is required. [Article III Section 11.3]
