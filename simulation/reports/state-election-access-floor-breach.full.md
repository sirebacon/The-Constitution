# State Election Access Is Cut Below the Democratic Floor
Scenario: state-election-access-floor-breach.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A state narrows access to state and local elections by ending automatic registration, eliminating early voting, and restricting absentee voting to narrow excuse categories. The scenario tests whether Article X's state-election voting-access floor now operates as a real constitutional backstop rather than leaving state election access largely outside the draft's enforceable protections.

- Day 1: VIOLATION - State officials attempt to run state elections under rules that reduce access below the constitutional floor by ending automatic registration for state elections, eliminating early voting, and restricting absentee voting to a narrow list of statutory excuses. [Article X Section 1.5 and Section 1.7A]
- Day 18: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that Article X now protects state-election access directly rather than relying only on federal-election rules or campaign-finance floors. [Article X Section 1.5 and Section 1.7A]
- Day 18: RESOLUTION - Federal courts blocked the restrictive state-election rules and ordered restoration of automatic registration, early voting, and no-excuse absentee access consistent with the constitutional floor. [Article X Section 1.5 and Section 1.7A]
