# Foreign Acquisition of Critical Infrastructure
Scenario: critical-infrastructure-coercion.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A foreign-state-linked company acquires controlling ownership of a major domestic telecommunications carrier. Congress invokes statutory national security review, finds a threat to constitutional governance, and orders divestiture. The scenario tests the new Art XIII §12 anchor for continuity and integrity standards governing critical civilian infrastructure.
- Day 1: EVENT - Meridian Global Networks acquires controlling ownership of the nation's second-largest domestic telecommunications carrier. The acquisition raises national security and constitutional continuity concerns because failure or hostile manipulation of the carrier could impair elections, official communications, and emergency response. Article XIII Section 12 now gives Congress explicit authority to establish continuity and integrity standards for critical infrastructure whose failure or hostile control would materially impair constitutional government. [Article XIII Section 12 and Article XVI]

- Day 75: OUTCOME - Congress acted on an explicit constitutional anchor.
