# Government Uses Internet Access as Political Punishment
Scenario: political-internet-access-punishment.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Local officials order a municipal broadband authority to throttle and repeatedly interrupt internet access for an opposition political organization after it criticizes the government. The scenario tests whether Article V's internet-access guarantee has real bite against political punishment or coercion.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must order prompt restoration of nondiscriminatory internet access and bar the politically punitive restriction by day 11. [Article V Section 10.6 and Section 2.6]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Government officials deny, throttle, or otherwise degrade internet access for a municipal opposition political organization because of political views or associations. Article V forbids using internet access as punishment, political coercion, or discrimination based on political viewpoint. [Article V Section 10.6 and Section 2.6]
- Day 6: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that the right to internet access is not symbolic. Government may not use throttling or denial of access as a political punishment or coercive tool. [Article V Section 10.6 and Section 2.6]
- Day 6: RESOLUTION - Federal courts ordered prompt restoration of nondiscriminatory internet access and barred the politically punitive restriction. [Article V Section 10.6 and Section 2.6]
