# Systemically Important Bank Faces Criminal Referral
Scenario: systemically-important-bank-criminal-case.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A politically connected systemically important bank conceals large-scale money laundering and fraudulent books-and-records practices. The financial market regulator refers the matter to the Accountability Commission, which prosecutes despite claims that criminal enforcement would destabilize the economy. The scenario tests whether Article VIII's market-integrity architecture truly rejects a 'too big to prosecute' exemption.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Financial market regulator must complete the investigation, make formal criminal referrals, and seek emergency asset-preservation or clawback remedies if warranted by day 46. [Article VIII Section 2.5, Section 2.6, and Section 2.7]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Union Atlantic Bank concealed large-scale money laundering tied to transnational trafficking proceeds and falsified compliance reporting while senior executives misled regulators and investors about the bank's risk controls. The scenario tests whether Article VIII's market-integrity provisions permit direct enforcement even where the target institution is politically connected and systemically important. [Article VIII Section 2.1, Section 2.5, Section 2.6, and Section 2.7]
- Day 35: OBLIGATION - Accountability Commission must decide whether to prosecute the referred criminal conduct without regard to the institution's size, political influence, or claimed systemic importance by day 65. [Article VIII Section 2.5 and Section 2.6]
- Day 35: OUTCOME - The regulator treated Union Atlantic Bank's size as irrelevant to criminal referral. The constitutional rule against 'too big to prosecute' behavior is now directly engaged. [Article VIII Section 2.5 and Section 2.6]
- Day 35: RESOLUTION - Financial market regulator completed the investigation, referred the criminal conduct to the Accountability Commission, and sought emergency judicial asset-preservation and executive-compensation clawback remedies. [Article VIII Section 2.5, Section 2.6, and Section 2.7]
- Day 58: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that Article VIII already has a meaningful constitutional foothold against politically protected large-institution misconduct. The financial market regulator and Accountability Commission can pursue criminal conduct without any constitutional exemption for institutions deemed too large or connected to prosecute. [Article VIII Section 2.5 and Section 2.6]
- Day 58: RESOLUTION - Accountability Commission filed charges against the institution and responsible executives, rejecting claims that systemic importance justified nonprosecution. [Article VIII Section 2.5 and Section 2.6]
