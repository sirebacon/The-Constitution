# Emergency Near Election Day
Scenario: emergency-near-election.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - President declares a national emergency. [Article III Section 5.1]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - President must submit the emergency declaration to the Regional Assembly by day 2. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Regional Assembly must approve or reject the emergency declaration by day 30. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The President declares an emergency shortly before a federal election and uses it to restrict voting access. The scenario tests emergency review, election-protection rules, and judicial timing.
- Day 1: RESOLUTION - President submitted the emergency declaration to the Regional Assembly. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 2: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must review the emergency-based election restriction on an expedited basis by day 5. [Article I Section 3.4 and Article V Section 14.2]
- Day 2: VIOLATION - Emergency measures restrict access to polling places, early voting, or certification. [Article I Section 3.4 and Article V Section 14.2]
- Day 4: RESOLUTION - Federal courts blocked the emergency-based restriction on election access. [Article I Section 3.4 and Article V Section 14.2]
- Day 31: BOTTLENECK - Regional Assembly failed to approve or reject the emergency declaration by day 30 under Article III Section 5.4. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 31: OUTCOME - The emergency declaration lapses automatically at the end of day 30, the Chief Justice must issue a public certificate of lapse, and all emergency-dependent measures become void unless separately authorized by ordinary law. [Article III Section 5.4]
