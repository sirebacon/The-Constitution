# Budget Deadlock — Automatic Continuing Resolution
Scenario: budget-deadlock-cr.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - Congress has not enacted appropriations legislation by October 1. The automatic continuing resolution takes effect at 98% of prior-year enacted levels. [Article II Section 12.2]
- Day 0: OUTCOME - The government may not shut down for lack of appropriations. The automatic continuing resolution is self-executing and requires no further legislative or executive action to take effect. [Article II Section 12.2]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Congress fails to pass appropriations legislation by October 1. The automatic continuing resolution takes effect at 98% of prior-year enacted levels under Article II Section 12.2. The executive branch then attempts to manipulate funding ratios under the CR — withholding funds from disfavored programs and redirecting them to preferred ones. The scenario tests whether courts can block this manipulation on an expedited basis.
- Day 5: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must review the executive branch's selective CR funding manipulation on an expedited basis by day 10. [Article II Section 12.2]
- Day 5: VIOLATION - Executive branch attempts to alter funding ratios, withhold appropriated funds, or selectively apply funding levels under the automatic continuing resolution. [Article II Section 12.2]
- Day 10: RESOLUTION - Federal courts issued an expedited order blocking the executive branch's selective application of the automatic continuing resolution. [Article II Section 12.2]
