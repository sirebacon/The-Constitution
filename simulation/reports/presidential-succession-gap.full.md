## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - President becomes incapacitated and unable to discharge presidential duties. [Article III Section 11 and Section 12]
- Day 0: EVENT - Vice President is also incapacitated and unable to assume presidential duties. Succession advances to the Speaker of the House under Article III Section 12.2. [Article III Section 12.2]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Speaker of the House must assume the office of Acting President with the limited powers of Article III Section 12.4 by day 1. [Article III Section 12.2]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Vice President must assume the powers and duties of the President as Acting President by day 1. [Article III Section 11.2 and Section 12.1]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The President and Vice President become simultaneously unable to serve. The succession chain advances to the Speaker of the House under Article III Section 12.2. The Speaker assumes the office of Acting President with the limited powers specified in Article III Section 12.4, which prohibits an unelected successor from exercising the pardon power, withdrawing from ratified treaties, declaring a national emergency, removing Senate-confirmed officials without cause, or making recess appointments without approval of a majority of both chambers. This scenario is expected to resolve cleanly — it tests that the succession chain functions and that its limits automatically apply.
- Day 1: RESOLUTION - Speaker of the House assumed the office of Acting President; Article III Section 12.4 limitations apply — no pardon power, no treaty withdrawal, no emergency declaration, no removal of confirmed officials without cause, no recess appointments without majority approval of both chambers.
