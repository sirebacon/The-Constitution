# Regional Assembly Recess Evasion Fails Under Section 15A Continuity
Scenario: ra-recess-evasion.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - President declares a national emergency. [Article III Section 5.1]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - President must submit the emergency declaration to the Regional Assembly by day 2. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Regional Assembly must approve or reject the emergency declaration by day 30. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The President declares a national emergency and submits it to the Regional Assembly on time. Rather than hold the constitutionally required approval vote, Regional Assembly leadership adjourns into recess on day 10 in an effort to run out the 30-day clock. A member files a Section 15A mandatory scheduling motion; the presiding officer still fails to bring it forward while the chamber is in recess. An affected party then files for mandamus in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia. The court orders the Regional Assembly to reconvene and hold the emergency vote, and the chamber approves the declaration before day 30. This scenario tests whether Article II Section 15A.1(e)-(f) and Article III Section 5.4 defeat recess-based deadline evasion.
- Day 1: RESOLUTION - President submitted the emergency declaration to the Regional Assembly. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 10: EVENT - Regional Assembly leadership attempts to run out the emergency-approval deadline by entering recess instead of holding the constitutionally required vote. [Article II Section 15A.1(e)-(f) and Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 10: OUTCOME - Any member of the Regional Assembly may file a mandatory scheduling motion, and any affected party may seek mandamus in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia if recess is used to block the vote. [Article II Section 15A.1(d)-(f)]
- Day 10: VIOLATION - Regional Assembly leadership attempts to run out the emergency-approval deadline by entering recess instead of holding the constitutionally required vote. [Article II Section 15A.1(e)-(f) and Article III Section 5.4]

- Day 11: OBLIGATION - Regional Assembly presiding officer must bring the Section 15A mandatory scheduling motion to a floor vote by day 14. [Article II Section 15A.1(b)]
- Day 14: BOTTLENECK - Regional Assembly presiding officer failed to bring the mandatory scheduling motion to a floor vote under Article II Section 15A.1(b). [Article II Section 15A.1(b)]
- Day 14: OBLIGATION - U.S. District Court for D.C. must rule on the mandamus petition and order scheduling of the required vote by day 19. [Article II Section 15A.1(d)]
- Day 15: EVENT - An affected party files for mandamus in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia to compel scheduling of the required vote. [Article II Section 15A.1(d)]
- Day 19: OBLIGATION - Regional Assembly must hold the emergency declaration vote as ordered by the court by day 26. [Article II Section 15A.1(d) and Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 19: RESOLUTION - U.S. District Court for D.C. issued a writ of mandamus ordering the Regional Assembly to schedule and hold the emergency declaration vote. [Article II Section 15A.1(d)]
- Day 23: RESOLUTION - Regional Assembly approved the emergency declaration following court-ordered scheduling. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 23: RESOLUTION - Regional Assembly held the required emergency declaration vote in compliance with the court order. [Article II Section 15A.1(d) and Article III Section 5.4]
