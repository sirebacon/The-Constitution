# State Denies Parental Recognition Based on Family Structure
Scenario: family-status-discrimination-parental-recognition.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - A state refuses to recognize the parental status of a lawfully formed same-sex married couple and treats one parent as a legal stranger to the child solely because of the sex and family structure of the parents. The scenario tests whether Article V protects family autonomy and parental recognition from discriminatory government interference.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal or state courts must restore lawful parental recognition and enjoin enforcement of the discriminatory family-status rule by day 22. [Article V Section 4.3 and Section 7.2]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - State officials refuse to recognize the parental status of a married same-sex couple raising a child born during the marriage solely because of the sex, gender, or family structure of the parents. Article V protects family formation and parental recognition from unjustified government interference and bars discriminatory classifications affecting family status. [Article V Section 4.1, Section 4.3, and Section 7.2]
- Day 14: OUTCOME - The scenario validates that Article V protects family autonomy in substance, not only marriage in name. The state may not deny legal parental recognition to an otherwise lawful family on discriminatory grounds. [Article V Section 4.3 and Section 7.2]
- Day 14: RESOLUTION - Federal or state courts restored parental recognition, barred enforcement of the discriminatory family-status rule, and held that family decisions may not be denied legal effect because of the sex, gender, or sexual orientation of the parents. [Article V Section 4.3 and Section 7.2]
