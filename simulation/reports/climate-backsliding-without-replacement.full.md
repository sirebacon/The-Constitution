# Climate Backsliding Without Constitutionally Sufficient Replacement
Scenario: climate-backsliding-without-replacement.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The executive branch rolls back major climate protections without simultaneously replacing them with measures constitutionally sufficient in aggregate. Courts declare a violation under Art XVIII §1.2, order a remedial plan, and retain jurisdiction. The political branches adopt a sufficient plan. Tests the anti-backsliding rule and the boundary between judicial declaration and judicial prescription of policy instruments.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must declare the climate-protection backsliding a constitutional violation and require the government to submit a constitutionally sufficient remedial plan within a defined period by day 61. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Government carries out administrative rollback of major greenhouse-gas regulations without replacement measures without simultaneously replacing those protections with measures constitutionally sufficient in aggregate. Article XVIII Section 1.2 prohibits knowingly and materially dismantling existing climate protections without constitutionally sufficient replacement. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 55: OBLIGATION - Congress and Executive branch must adopt and submit a constitutionally sufficient remedial climate plan within the court's deadline by day 235. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 55: OUTCOME - Courts may declare a climate-floor violation and require a remedial plan, but may not prescribe emissions targets, technology choices, tax measures, or line-item appropriations. The political branches choose among materially sufficient policy alternatives. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 55: RESOLUTION - Federal courts declared a constitutional violation and ordered the government to submit a constitutionally sufficient remedial climate plan within 180 days; retained jurisdiction to review the plan's adequacy. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 220: OUTCOME - The constitutional floor is restored. Article XVIII Section 1.2 enforcement produced a remedial plan through the political branches without judicial prescription of specific policy instruments. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
- Day 220: RESOLUTION - Congress and Executive branch adopted and submitted a remedial climate plan that the court found constitutionally sufficient in aggregate. [Article XVIII Section 1.2 and Section 1.4]
