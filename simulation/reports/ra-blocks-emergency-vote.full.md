# Regional Assembly Blocks Emergency Vote — Section 15A Enforcement
Scenario: ra-blocks-emergency-vote.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - President declares a national emergency. [Article III Section 5.1]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - President must submit the emergency declaration to the Regional Assembly by day 2. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Regional Assembly must approve or reject the emergency declaration by day 30. [Article III Section 5.4]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The President declares a national emergency and submits it to the Regional Assembly on time. Regional Assembly leadership refuses to place the declaration on the floor calendar. A member files a mandatory scheduling motion under Article II Section 15A; the presiding officer fails to act on it. An affected party petitions the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia for a writ of mandamus. The court orders scheduling within its 5-day window. The Regional Assembly holds the approval vote at day 15, within the 30-day constitutional deadline. This scenario tests whether the Section 15A enforcement chain closes the Regional Assembly emergency review bottleneck that the existing emergency scenarios expose.
