# Sensitive-Office Nationality Restriction Expands into General Civic Disability
Scenario: sensitive-office-restriction-overreach.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Congress imposes a blanket dual-citizenship exclusion for a broad class of federal offices and tries to justify it as a sensitive-office safeguard. The scenario tests whether Article IX forces such restrictions to remain narrowly tailored exceptions rather than a general hierarchy of citizenship or nationality.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must invalidate or narrowly construe the overbroad sensitive-office restriction so that it does not create a general hierarchy of citizenship or nationality by day 15. [Article IX Section 4.2, Section 4.5, and Section 5.3]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - Congress imposes a blanket exclusion of all dual citizens on eligibility for a wide class of senior federal offices without showing that the rule is narrowly tailored to an office involving exceptional national-security responsibility. Article IX permits targeted nationality, loyalty, or disclosure rules for especially sensitive offices, but not overbroad civic hierarchy by implication. [Article IX Section 4.2, Section 4.5, and Section 5.3]
- Day 10: RESOLUTION - Federal courts held that the restriction was overbroad, limited any valid nationality safeguards to genuinely exceptional national-security offices, and barred use of the rule as a general civic disability. [Article IX Section 4.2, Section 4.5, and Section 5.3]
