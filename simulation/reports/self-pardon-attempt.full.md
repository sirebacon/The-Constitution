# Presidential Self-Pardon Attempt
Scenario: self-pardon-attempt.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: EVENT - Accountability Commission indicts the sitting President. Trial is stayed until the President leaves office; pre-trial proceedings continue. [Article III Section 15.5]
- Day 0: OBLIGATION - Accountability Commission must continue pre-trial proceedings while the President remains in office. [Article III Section 15.5]
- Day 0: SCENARIO - The Accountability Commission indicts the sitting President for criminal conduct. The President responds by issuing a self-pardon. The scenario tests the absolute prohibition on self-pardons under Article III Section 7.2(a) and confirms that the Accountability Commission's prosecution continues notwithstanding the void pardon claim.
- Day 5: OUTCOME - The self-pardon is void. Presidential self-pardons are absolutely prohibited under Article III Section 7.2(a) and constitute a no-immunity category under Section 15.3. The Accountability Commission's prosecution continues without interruption. [Article III Section 7.2(a) and Section 15.3]
- Day 5: VIOLATION - President issues a pardon for their own criminal conduct. [Article III Section 7.2(a) and Section 15.3]
- Day 10: RESOLUTION - Accountability Commission proceeded with pre-trial proceedings notwithstanding the void self-pardon claim. [Article III Section 15.5]
