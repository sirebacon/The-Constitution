# Coordinated Private Infrastructure Cutoff of Lawful Opposition Organization
Scenario: private-chokepoint-election-cutoff.json

## Timeline
- Day 0: SCENARIO - Payment processors and hosting firms jointly cut off a lawful opposition organization during a federal election period. No formal government coercion is proved. The scenario tests whether the current Constitution provides a direct remedy against coordinated private chokepoint exclusion absent state action or foreign control.
- Day 1: OBLIGATION - Federal courts must determine whether existing constitutional provisions or implementing law provide a direct remedy for coordinated private exclusion of a lawful political organization during an election by day 15. [Article V Section 2.6; Article VII; Article XIII]
- Day 1: VIOLATION - dominant payment processors and hosting firms jointly cut off service to a lawful opposition organization operating nationally during a federal election period. No formal government coercion is proved. The harm is politically severe, but the current Constitution has no clearly defined direct remedy for synchronized private exclusion by dominant civic infrastructure intermediaries absent state action, foreign control, or campaign-finance violation. [Article V Section 2.6; Article VII; Article XIII]
- Day 10: OUTCOME - This scenario exposes a real under-tested gap. The draft is stronger against government censorship and foreign control than against purely private chokepoint exclusion of lawful political actors during an election. [Article V Section 2.6; Article VII; Article XIII]
- Day 10: RESOLUTION - Federal courts found no clear direct constitutional remedy because the exclusion was carried out by private firms without proven state coercion, foreign control, or a violation of an existing statutory duty. [Article V Section 2.6; Article VII; Article XIII]
