# Vacancy And Succession

This visual guide shows what happens when a federal office becomes vacant because of death, resignation, removal, or permanent incapacity.

## How To Read This Guide

Each table covers one office type. Use the category buttons to focus on Congress, the presidency, the vice presidency, or the federal judiciary.

## What This Guide Covers

- House vacancies and special elections
- Regional Assembly vacancies and temporary appointments
- presidential succession
- vice-presidential replacement
- Supreme Court and lower-court continuity through Senior Justices and Senior Judges

## What This Guide Does Not Do

This guide is an orientation layer, not the constitutional text itself.

For exact wording and legal detail, read:

- [Article I](articles/I-electoral-system.md) — congressional vacancies and special elections
- [Article III](articles/III-executive.md) — presidential succession and vice-presidential vacancy
- [Article IV](articles/IV-judiciary.md) — judicial continuity, vacancies, and senior service
