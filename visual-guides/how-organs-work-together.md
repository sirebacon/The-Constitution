# How Constitutional Organs Work Together

This visual guide explains what each constitutional organ does, how they relate to each other, and how the design prevents any single organ — or any official — from becoming dominant.

## How To Read This Guide

Each card covers one organ or one category of cross-organ relationships. Use the category buttons to focus on the Electoral Commission, Accountability Commission, courts and Judicial Conduct Board, or the cross-organ checks that hold the system together.

## What This Guide Covers

- Electoral Commission: what it runs, who it reports to, and how it is protected
- Accountability Commission: scope of investigations, referral triggers, and why it cannot be shut down by the official it investigates
- Courts and Judicial Conduct Board: how they review all branch actions and handle judicial discipline
- Cross-organ checks: appointment separation, annual public reports, and why institutional sabotage is itself a constitutional offense

## What This Guide Does Not Do

This guide is an orientation layer, not the constitutional text itself.
