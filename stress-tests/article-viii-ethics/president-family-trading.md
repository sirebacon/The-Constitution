# Test: President Family Trading on Non-Public Information

## Scenario

The President's spouse sells a large defense-sector position shortly before a classified procurement cancellation becomes public. The administration argues that the trade was lawful because the President personally did not execute it and the spouse's account technically held a diversified portfolio with one concentrated side position.

## Articles Implicated

- Article VIII Section 1
- Article III presidential accountability provisions

## Questions

1. Do the conflict and trading rules reach covered family members?
2. Is a side position within an otherwise diversified account enough to trigger the rule?
3. What remedies exist if profit is derived from non-public official information?
4. Does the Constitution make removal, disgorgement, or prosecution available?
5. Is the line between permitted broad investment and disguised concentrated exposure clear enough?

## Expected Outcome

- Covered family members are within the rule.
- Concentrated positions that create a conflict are not saved by being housed inside a broader account.
- Profit derived from non-public information may not be retained.
- Disgorgement and other serious remedies are available.

## Stress Focus

- Abuse resistance
- Draft discipline

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- The family-member and non-public-information rules are hard to evade.

Fail if:

- A formalistic account-structure argument defeats the constitutional purpose.
