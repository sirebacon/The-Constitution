# Test: State Democratic Backsliding

## Scenario

A state government cancels local elections in opposition-controlled cities, removes judicial tenure protections, and redraws election administration so that the ruling party directly controls certification. Federal courts issue ordinary rulings for two years, but the state keeps adapting around them.

## Articles Implicated

- Article X Section 1.5
- Article X Section 1.6
- Article V rights floor

## Questions

1. What constitutional standard defines the state's failure?
2. Who may trigger the enforcement mechanism?
3. What sequence follows after a Supreme Court finding?
4. What happens to the rights of the state's residents during representation suspension?
5. Does the Constitution make expulsion possible?

## Expected Outcome

- The state is violating the democratic floor.
- The United States or another state's legislature or executive may petition.
- The sequence is Supreme Court finding, congressional remedy, automatic interim federal administration of federal elections if Congress misses the remedy deadline, then a mandatory suspension vote, followed by automatic suspension if noncompliance still persists.
- Residents retain constitutional rights even if representation is suspended.
- The state cannot be expelled from the Union.

## Stress Focus

- Enforceability
- Abuse resistance
- Draft discipline

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- The enforcement sequence is easy to follow.
- The text clearly punishes the state government without stripping resident rights.
- A missed congressional remedy does not leave federal elections in the violating state unprotected.

Fail if:

- The remedy depends on guesswork about who acts next.
- Suspension mechanics appear to target residents rather than institutions.
