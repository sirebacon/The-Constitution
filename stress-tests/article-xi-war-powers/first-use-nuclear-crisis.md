# Test: First-Use Nuclear Crisis

## Scenario

The President claims a hostile state is preparing a catastrophic conventional strike and orders first use of nuclear weapons before any nuclear weapon has been launched at the United States or an ally. Congressional leaders are notified minutes before the proposed launch.

## Articles Implicated

- Article XI Section 3

## Questions

1. Can the President order first use unilaterally?
2. What confirmations and findings are required?
3. What role does congressional leadership have?
4. What changes if there is a confirmed incoming nuclear strike instead?
5. Is the text clear enough under extreme time pressure?

## Expected Outcome

- First use cannot proceed unilaterally.
- Secretary-level confirmation and DNI findings are required.
- Congressional leadership can block first use absent the required override.
- A confirmed incoming nuclear strike shifts the scenario into the response rule.
- The text should remain legible even under urgent conditions.

## Stress Focus

- Clarity
- Enforceability

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- The first-use rule and response rule are clearly distinct.

Fail if:

- There is meaningful confusion about whether ordinary first use and retaliatory response are governed by the same process.
