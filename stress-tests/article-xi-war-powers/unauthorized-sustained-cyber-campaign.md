# Test: Unauthorized Sustained Cyber Campaign

## Scenario

After a serious foreign cyber intrusion, the President orders an immediate counter-operation and then expands it into a month-long campaign degrading the foreign state's power grid, logistics, and military networks without a declaration of war or AUMF.

## Articles Implicated

- Article XI Section 1
- Article XI Section 2
- Article XIV Section 5

## Questions

1. Which actions qualify as defensive cyber response?
2. At what point does the operation become military force requiring authorization?
3. Can the administration re-label the operation as gray-zone response under Article XIV?
4. What deadline governs continued operations?
5. What remedy applies if authorization never comes?

## Expected Outcome

- Immediate narrow defensive action may be lawful.
- Expanded sustained offensive cyber operations become military force under Article XI.
- Article XIV cannot be used to evade Article XI once the response amounts to military force.
- Authorization is required within the constitutional window.
- A withdrawal order follows if authorization is not granted.

## Stress Focus

- Cross-article coherence
- Abuse resistance

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- Article XI and XIV draw a clear boundary.
- The trigger for withdrawal is easy to identify.

Fail if:

- The President can plausibly bypass war powers by rebranding the campaign.
