# Test: Dark-Money Layering Through Intermediaries

## Scenario

A wealthy donor routes money through a nonprofit, then an LLC, then a political committee, with each layer disclosing only the immediately prior entity. The campaign claims all statutory forms were properly filed and no one can trace the original human source in time for the election.

## Articles Implicated

- Article VII disclosure and tracing provisions
- Article VII Electoral Commission authority

## Questions

1. Does the constitutional rule require tracing back to the original human donor?
2. Can intermediary compliance substitute for source disclosure?
3. Which institution has enforcement authority?
4. Does the Constitution produce a timely enough answer before the election?
5. Is there an obvious shell-entity loophole left open?

## Expected Outcome

- The original human source must be traceable.
- Layered intermediary disclosure alone is insufficient.
- The Electoral Commission and related enforcement mechanisms have authority.
- The structure should support pre-election transparency rather than only post-election punishment.

## Stress Focus

- Enforceability
- Abuse resistance

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- The anti-conduit principle is unmistakable.

Fail if:

- A shell structure can plausibly defeat the constitutional floor.
