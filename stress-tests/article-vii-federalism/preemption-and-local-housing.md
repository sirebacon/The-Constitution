# Test: Preemption and Local Housing Rule

## Scenario

A city adopts a dense-housing ordinance to address a regional shortage. The state legislature nullifies it, claiming statewide uniformity. The city argues the matter is predominantly local and the state is preempting for ideological reasons rather than a genuine statewide interest.

## Articles Implicated

- Article VII Section 8
- Article VII Section 4

## Questions

1. Is housing purely local, purely statewide, or mixed under the text?
2. What burden must the state meet to override local action?
3. Does the Constitution permit statewide minimum standards?
4. Is the standard specific enough for a court to apply?
5. Does the article create too much room for judicial improvisation?

## Expected Outcome

- Housing is treated as an area where both local and statewide interests may exist.
- The state must identify a genuine statewide interest rather than a bare preference.
- Statewide minimum standards are permitted.
- The city should have a colorable constitutional claim if the override is pretextual.

## Stress Focus

- Clarity
- Enforceability

## Pass/Fail Notes

Pass if:

- A court can explain the local versus statewide line without inventing a new constitution.

Fail if:

- The text leaves the override rule too open-ended to administer.
