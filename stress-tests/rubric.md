# Stress Test Rubric

Score each scenario from 1 to 5 on these dimensions.

## Scale

- `5` Clear and durable
- `4` Strong but needs small tightening
- `3` Usable but ambiguous or awkward
- `2` Weak or difficult to apply
- `1` Fails badly

## Dimensions

### Clarity

Can a careful reader identify the governing text and answer the scenario without substantial guesswork?

### Enforceability

Can the institution named in the text actually apply the rule on the required timeline?

### Abuse Resistance

Does the text block the most obvious bad-faith workaround?

### Cross-Article Coherence

If more than one article is implicated, do they fit together cleanly?

### Draft Discipline

Does the Constitution answer the problem at the right level of abstraction, without collapsing into statutory detail or leaving a fatal gap?

## Outcome Guide

- `22-25`
  Strong pass. Protect from unnecessary churn.
- `18-21`
  Pass with targeted cleanup.
- `13-17`
  Needs revision.
- `12 or below`
  Structural problem. Rewrite or redesign.

## Failure Notes

When a scenario scores poorly, record:

- The clause that created uncertainty
- The competing readings
- The practical consequence of that ambiguity
- Whether the fix belongs in constitutional text or in notes/statute
