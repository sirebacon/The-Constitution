# Presidential System Hardening Plan

This document translates the current simulator and scorecard into a practical hardening plan for the draft's presidential system. The goal is not to redesign the regime as parliamentary government. The goal is to keep presidentialism while reducing its known failure modes: deadlock, personalist abuse, procedural sabotage, and overengineered enforcement.

---

## Strategic View

The current draft is already doing the most important thing a modern presidential constitution must do: it replaces norm dependence with clear constitutional consequence chains.

The remaining work is narrower:

1. Separate true text gaps from acceptable political-risk scenarios.
2. Keep enforcement institutions strong without making them too dense.
3. Simplify the constitutional text so the anti-abuse architecture remains readable and durable.

This is best treated as three parallel workstreams:

- legislative nonperformance and deadline/fallback review
- enforcement architecture review
- draft-discipline compression

---

## Workstream 1 — Legislative Nonperformance

### Objective

Make sure missed legislative action creates political blame, not constitutional paralysis.

### Core Rule

Where the Constitution already has a strong automatic consequence, keep it. Do not “fix” every missed vote just because the simulator flags it as a bottleneck. A bottleneck is only a text problem when inaction still blocks constitutional closure or creates an exploitable ambiguity.

### Keep As Designed

These are stress points, but not current text gaps:

| Scenario | Missed Action | Existing Backstop | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Unauthorized military action | Congress misses day-30 authorization | Article XVI already treats nonaction as nonauthorization and triggers withdrawal logic | Keep |
| Unauthorized military action | House misses impeachment vote | Article III Section 10.2A already transmits the matter automatically to the Regional Assembly | Keep |
| President obstructs investigation | House misses expedited impeachment vote | Same Article III Section 10.2A fallback | Keep |
| Emergency extension without real review | Regional Assembly misses day-30 review vote | Article III Section 5.4 already causes lapse and voids continued measures | Keep |
| Emergency near election | Regional Assembly misses day-30 review vote | Same automatic lapse rule | Keep |
| Contested incapacity declaration | Congress misses resolution deadline | Article III already returns presidential powers absent sustained incapacity | Keep |
| PMC substitution | House misses expedited impeachment vote | Same automatic transmission fallback | Keep |
| State democratic backsliding | Congress misses remedial deadline | Article X already starts federal election administration and later hard suspension | Keep |
| RA recess evasion | Presiding officer misses mandatory scheduling motion | Article II Section 15A continuity rules already convert the tactic into a visible violation while preserving the emergency path | Keep |

### Tighten or Review

These are narrower and worth real follow-up:

| Area | Current Issue | Best Next Move |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Accountability Commission certification delay | Institutional delay can still slow the start of an impeachment path even after the member-certification fallback | Validate the current fallback in more scenarios and keep the certification override narrow |
| Constitutional Organs setup delay | The transition bridge now works, but the permanent architecture should read more cleanly with Article XIX | Reconcile Article XII and Article XIX so the organ-startup path feels unified rather than patched |
| Coordinated absenteeism | The Constitution now treats this as obstruction, but scenario coverage is still limited | Add more scenarios where members hide sabotage behind leave, travel, or chamber custom |

### Done Standard

This workstream is done when every remaining missed-vote scenario falls into one of two buckets:

- acceptable political nonperformance with a clear automatic consequence
- genuine text gap with a narrow documented fix

## Workstream 2 — Enforcement Architecture

### Objective

Keep Article IV, Article VIII, Article XII, and related enforcement rules strong without letting them become mini-codes.

### Main Risks

- too many layered procedures
- overlapping jurisdictions
- commission and court provisions drifting back into administrative-code detail
- anti-abuse systems becoming hard to follow in one read

### What to Do

#### Article IV — Judiciary

Keep the current structure narrow:

- delay notice at six months
- temporary lower-court effect in expedited cases
- party-level finality at twelve months in expedited cases
- conduct review for strategic abuse

Do not broaden these rules into a general “Supreme Court management code.” The remaining task is targeted scenario validation, not more doctrinal expansion unless a new scenario exposes a real gap.

#### Article XII — Constitutional Organs

Keep the article self-contained and lean:

- each organ should state purpose, core powers, limits, quorum, continuity, and removal in one read
- appointment and vacancy rules should remain intelligible without heavy external lookup
- internal sabotage and boycott rules should remain narrow and continuity-focused

The next task is consistency review with Article XIX, not rebuilding the article again.

#### Article VIII and Article XIII

These should continue to move toward durable constitutional floors rather than sector-specific regulatory codes.

Focus:

- ethics principles over compliance detail
- anti-corruption floors over administrative elaboration
- revolving-door and agency-independence rules at the level of constitutional principle, not detailed market-regulation machinery

### Done Standard

This workstream is done when an informed reader can trace each enforcement path from trigger to consequence without needing to assemble half a dozen cross-references.

---

## Workstream 3 — Draft Discipline Compression

### Objective

Keep the constitutional architecture strong while reducing code-like drafting.

### Compression Rule

Retain only what is constitutionally necessary:

- authority
- duty
- limit
- consequence

Push everything else toward law unless removing it would reopen an abuse gap.

### Highest-Value Compression Targets

| Article | Why It Matters | What to Trim |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Article VII — Campaign Finance | Strong policy, but still the clearest draft-discipline outlier | Operational detail, parameter lists, and compliance-style mechanics |
| Article VIII — Government Ethics | Still at risk of drifting into regulator-specific detail | Sector-specific detail that can live in law |
| Article XIII — Federal Agencies | Most likely to read like a code book if left unattended | Administrative implementation detail |
| Article XIX — Ratification and Transition | Transition clauses can easily become operational law | Open-ended corrective-legislation language and implementation-level detail |
| Article II — Legislature | Deadline and continuity machinery is necessary but can sprawl | Repetition, internal process detail, and any rule better stated once elsewhere |

### Done Standard

This workstream is done when the document still closes abuse gaps but reads like foundational law rather than a constitutionalized operations manual.

---

## Recommended Order

1. Finish the deadline/fallback audit and clearly mark which bottlenecks are acceptable residual political risk.
2. Review non-legislative bottlenecks:
   - Accountability Commission certification delay
   - Constitutional Organs setup delay
   - judicial delay scenarios
3. Run a consistency pass on Article XII / Article XIX / Article IV rather than adding new machinery by reflex.
4. Run a draft-discipline pass on Articles VII, VIII, XIII, XIX, and then II.

---

## Bottom Line

The current design does not need a regime change. It needs disciplined hardening.

The right approach is:

- keep strong automatic consequences
- tighten only the few places where delay still buys real leverage
- keep enforcement bodies legible and bounded
- keep compressing detail so presidentialism does not require an unreadable constitution

That is how this draft becomes a defensible, modern presidential constitution rather than a merely patched version of the old U.S. model.
