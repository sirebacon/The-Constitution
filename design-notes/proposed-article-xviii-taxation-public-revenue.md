# Proposed Article XVIII — Taxation and Public Revenue

**Status:** `[ PROPOSAL ]`

---

## Purpose

This proposed article separates taxation and revenue authority from the broader budget and appropriations framework. It is meant to state the constitutional principles governing how public money is raised, without folding those principles into shutdown, debt, or appropriations mechanics.

It should not be integrated into the operative draft until overlapping revenue provisions are migrated from [articles/II-legislature.md](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/II-legislature.md).

---

## Section 1 — General Principle

**1.1** Public revenue may be raised only under law.

**1.2** Taxation is an instrument of democratic self-government and must remain publicly accountable, transparent, and subject to constitutional control.

**1.3** No tax system may be structured or administered to create concealed tribute, arbitrary favoritism, or practical disenfranchisement inconsistent with this Constitution.

---

## Section 2 — Power to Tax

**2.1** Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes, duties, imposts, and excises for public purposes consistent with this Constitution.

**2.2** Tax laws must be enacted by Congress and may not be created, repealed, or materially altered by executive action except as authorized by law.

**2.3** Revenue measures must originate in the House of Representatives as provided elsewhere in this Constitution.

**2.4** Congress may design progressive, proportional, or mixed systems of taxation, subject to the equal-protection, due-process, anti-corruption, and anti-discrimination constraints of this Constitution.

---

## Section 3 — Revenue Integrity

**3.1** Congress may require public reporting, anti-evasion rules, audit mechanisms, and enforcement systems sufficient to preserve the integrity of the revenue system.

**3.2** No officer may collect, waive, redirect, or selectively withhold taxes except as authorized by law.

**3.3** No tax exemption, subsidy, or comparable revenue preference may be used as a disguised means of corruption, partisan retaliation, or circumvention of constitutional limitations.

---

## Section 4 — Uniformity and Fair Administration

**4.1** Congress shall provide by law for uniform and reviewable procedures for tax assessment, collection, challenge, and refund.

**4.2** No person may be deprived of property through tax administration without due process of law.

**4.3** Tax administration may not be weaponized against political opponents, dissenters, journalists, or disfavored groups.

---

## Section 5 — Construction

**5.1** This Article shall be construed to preserve democratic control of public revenue, fair administration, and resistance to concealed extraction or partisan misuse.

**5.2** No ambiguity in this Article shall be resolved in favor of executive tax nullification, discriminatory enforcement, or hidden systems of public finance.
