# Proposed Article XVI — Ratification and Transition

## Section 1 — Ratification

**1.1** This Constitution shall take effect upon ratification by conventions or legislatures in three-fourths of the states, as Congress shall determine by law.

**1.2** Ratification under this Section supersedes the prior Constitution of the United States except as expressly carried forward by this Article or by other provisions of this Constitution.

**1.3** Upon ratification, all federal and state officers shall remain bound to preserve, protect, and defend this Constitution in place of the prior Constitution.

---

## Section 2 — Continuity of Law

**2.1** All federal statutes, treaties, regulations, judicial judgments, and lawful obligations in force at the time of ratification remain in force unless they conflict with this Constitution, are superseded by laws enacted under it, or expire by their own terms.

**2.2** Existing offices, departments, courts, and agencies continue temporarily insofar as necessary to preserve legal continuity, but must be conformed to this Constitution within the periods provided by law or by this Article.

**2.3** No existing law shall be given effect after ratification in a manner inconsistent with any right, structural rule, or institutional limitation established by this Constitution.

---

## Section 3 — Carried-Forward and Superseded Constitutional Provisions

**3.1** Rights protections from the prior Constitution that are consistent with this Constitution are carried forward only to the extent incorporated into Article V or otherwise restated here.

**3.2** All prior constitutional provisions governing the structure, powers, composition, selection, terms, succession, or procedures of the federal branches are superseded by Articles I through IV and Articles VII through XV of this Constitution.

**3.3** All prior constitutional provisions inconsistent with direct national election of the President, the abolition of the Electoral College, the Regional Assembly, fixed Supreme Court terms, the Constitutional Organs, or the amendment framework of this Constitution are superseded.

**3.4** Congress shall by law publish an official transition schedule identifying the principal prior constitutional provisions carried forward, superseded, or restated, but the legal effect of this Constitution does not depend on that publication.

---

## Section 4 — First Elections and Terms

**4.1** Congress shall by law provide for the first federal elections held under this Constitution and for the orderly commencement of terms under the new constitutional order.

**4.2** The first elections under this Constitution must be conducted no later than one year after ratification unless this Constitution expressly provides otherwise.

**4.3** Holders of offices abolished or materially transformed by this Constitution shall continue temporarily only for the time strictly necessary to transfer authority to the offices created by this Constitution.

**4.4** Congress shall by law provide initial staggering, temporary seat allocation, and transitional succession arrangements necessary to establish the House of Representatives, the Regional Assembly, and the new election system, but no such law may alter the permanent constitutional design.

---

## Section 5 — Judicial and Institutional Transition

**5.1** Congress shall by law provide for the transition from the prior federal judiciary to the judicial structure established by Article IV, including the sequencing of terms, temporary service, and the establishment of lower-court terms consistent with that Article.

**5.2** The Constitutional Organs established by Article IX shall be constituted as soon as practicable after ratification and shall assume their constitutional functions on the timeline provided by law, but no later than one year after ratification unless impossible due to conditions identified publicly by the Supreme Court.

**5.3** Existing agencies and officers whose functions are reassigned under this Constitution shall continue only until those functions are lawfully transferred, replaced, or terminated consistent with this Constitution.

---

## Section 6 — Electoral and Executive Transition

**6.1** The lawful constitutional transfer of power to officers elected or confirmed under this Constitution is mandatory. No outgoing official may obstruct the establishment of the constitutional order or use the powers of an expiring office to frustrate transition.

**6.2** Congress shall provide public funding, access, records, security, and administrative support sufficient for constitutional transition.

**6.3** Obstruction of the ratified constitutional transition through force, fraud, abuse of official authority, or deliberate refusal to transfer lawful power is anti-subversion and grounds for removal, disqualification, and prosecution.

---

## Section 7 — Temporary Corrective Legislation

**7.1** For ten years after ratification, Congress may enact technical corrective legislation to resolve conflicts, omissions, or implementation defects arising from transition, provided such legislation is consistent with this Constitution and does not alter its permanent structure.

**7.2** The Supreme Court shall have expedited jurisdiction to review any claim that purported corrective legislation exceeds the authority of this Section.

---

## Section 8 — First Amendment Cycle

**8.1** Any structural amendment cooling-off period, transition limitation, or first-cycle rule established elsewhere in this Constitution shall be read in harmony with this Article.

**8.2** Rights expansions remain permitted on the timetable established by Article VIII.

---

## Section 9 — Construction

**9.1** This Article shall be construed to favor legal continuity, democratic legitimacy, and the prompt establishment of the constitutional order created by this Constitution.

**9.2** No ambiguity in transition shall be resolved in favor of preserving obsolete institutions, delaying constitutional implementation, or frustrating the lawful transfer of authority.
