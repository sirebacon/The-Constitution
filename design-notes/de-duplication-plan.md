# De-Duplication Plan

## Purpose

This plan identifies constitutional rules that are currently duplicated, partially duplicated, or split across multiple Articles in ways that weaken clarity, increase conflict risk, and make future revision more expensive. The goal is not to delete useful safeguards. The goal is to make each subject have one authoritative constitutional home, while preserving short application rules or cross-references where they still improve comprehension.

This is an execution plan, not a theory memo. Each pass below is designed to be implementable in order.

## Working Principles

**1. One authoritative home per subject.** General constitutional rules should live in the Article most directly devoted to that subject.

**2. Keep application-specific rules where they matter.** An Article may still contain a narrow office-specific or institution-specific application of a general principle.

**3. Replace duplication with cross-reference, not silence.** Where a rule is moved, leave a short pointer if the old location still needs to orient the reader.

**4. Preserve real safeguards.** Repeated protection is only a problem when it creates conflict or drift. Do not remove redundancy that functions as a distinct backstop.

**5. Separate mechanical moves from substantive revision.** First consolidate authority. Then tighten language. Do not blend both unless necessary.

## Pass 1: Fiscal De-Duplication

### Target Constitutional Homes

- Taxing power, revenue fairness, revenue integrity, and anti-weaponization principles belong in [Article XIV](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/XIV-taxation-public-revenue.md)
- Borrowing, debt, appropriations, budget continuity, off-budget prohibitions, classified spending, fiscal transparency, and emergency finance belong in [Article XV](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/XV-budget-public-credit-appropriations.md)
- Legislative role rules belong in [Article II](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/II-legislature.md)

### Keep / Move / Cross-Reference Decisions

**Keep in Article II**

- House origination rule for revenue and appropriations in Section 7
- General congressional power to legislate on taxation, borrowing, and expenditures in Section 9, but only as a structural allocation of power
- Budget timetable at a high level only if needed for legislative workflow

**Move from Article II to Article XV**

- Automatic continuing funding rule in Section 12.2
- Funding ratio specifics in Section 12.3, unless intentionally demoted to statute
- Fiscal-emergency deficit rule in Section 12.4
- Independent fiscal analysis and voting precondition rule in Section 12.5
- Debt-ceiling abolition rule in Section 12.6
- Off-budget prohibition in Section 12.7
- Budget publication and disclosure rules in Section 12.8
- Long-term projections in Section 12.9
- Performance reporting in Section 12.10, unless demoted to statute
- All classified-spending rules in Section 12A

**Move from Article II to Article XIV**

- Any remaining general taxation-principle language that is substantive rather than institutional

**Leave behind in Article II as short references**

- One short statement that Article XIV governs taxation and public revenue
- One short statement that Article XV governs appropriations, borrowing, continuity, and fiscal integrity

### Desired End State

After this pass, a reader should no longer treat Article II as the real fiscal article. Article II should explain who acts. Articles XIV and XV should explain the constitutional fiscal rules.

### Open Drafting Choices

- Whether the `98%` continuing-resolution ratio remains constitutional or moves to statute
- Whether program-performance reporting remains constitutional or becomes statutory implementation
- Whether the independent fiscal analysis body is constitutionally fixed or described more generally

## Pass 2: Citizenship and Eligibility De-Duplication

### Target Constitutional Homes

- Equal citizenship, domicile, overseas membership, citizenship administration, and general officeholding equality belong in [Article IX](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/IX-citizenship-membership.md)
- Election-specific procedures for federal voting remain in [Article I](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/I-electoral-system.md)
- Presidency-specific qualifications remain in [Article III](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/III-executive.md)

### Keep / Move / Cross-Reference Decisions

**Keep in Article I**

- Election administration mechanics for overseas ballots
- Electoral Commission administration rules needed specifically for federal election operation

**Move from Article I to Article IX**

- General constitutional principle that overseas citizens remain part of the national political community
- Domicile-assignment principle for citizens who have never established state domicile
- Any broad political-membership rule that is not purely electoral procedure

**Keep in Article III**

- Age requirement for President and Vice President
- Residency requirement for President and Vice President
- Presidency-specific sole-citizenship rule if retained
- Presidency-specific foreign-service and disclosure rules if retained

**Move from Article III to Article IX**

- General equal-citizenship baseline now stated in Section 2.2
- Any wording that reads like a general hierarchy between citizens by birth and naturalization

**Leave behind in Article I and Article III as short references**

- Article I should point to Article IX for the constitutional basis of overseas political membership and domicile
- Article III should point to Article IX for the general rule of equal citizenship and officeholding equality

### Desired End State

After this pass, Article IX should be the authoritative home of citizenship and national membership, while Articles I and III should contain only electoral or presidency-specific applications.

### Open Drafting Choices

- Whether to constitutionalize a clearer birthright/naturalization baseline
- Whether Article IX Section 5.2 should be narrowed so involuntary loss of citizenship is almost entirely excluded
- Whether the presidency-specific sole-citizenship rule should remain constitutional or be narrowed further

## Pass 3: Transition De-Duplication

### Target Constitutional Home

- General ratification, continuity, first-cycle implementation, institutional transition, anti-obstruction, and corrective-legislation rules belong in [Article XIX](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/XIX-ratification-transition.md)

### Keep / Move / Cross-Reference Decisions

**Keep in branch Articles only if genuinely branch-specific**

- Presidency-specific transfer-of-power obligations in [Article III](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/III-executive.md) if they regulate the outgoing and incoming President as such
- Judiciary-specific active-term conversion details in [Article IV](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/IV-judiciary.md) if they are too specific to general transition law
- Amendment-specific permanent rules in [Article XI](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/articles/XI-amendments.md)

**Move into Article XIX**

- General first-cycle timing rules that currently appear elsewhere
- General organ-constitution deadlines that are not unique to a single branch
- General anti-obstruction transition rules that currently duplicate executive-specific provisions
- Transition placeholders or restatement rules that still sit outside the transition article

**Leave behind as short references**

- Branch articles should point to Article XIX for general ratification and first-cycle transition
- Article XI should point to Article XIX for first-cycle implementation where appropriate

### Desired End State

After this pass, Article XIX should be the unquestioned constitutional home for transition. Other Articles should keep only genuinely branch-specific transition mechanics.

### Open Drafting Choices

- How much of Article IV Section 11 remains in the judiciary article versus Article XIX
- Whether Article III Section 13 remains fully separate as a standing presidential-transition article or is shortened and harmonized with Article XIX
- Whether Article XIX should own more of the initial Constitutional Organs timetable now partly reflected elsewhere

## Validation Checklist

After each pass:

1. `rg` the moved subject terms across the affected Articles and confirm only the intended home contains the full substantive rule.
2. Confirm the old location still reads coherently with the short replacement cross-reference.
3. Review [CONSTITUTION.md](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/CONSTITUTION.md) for any summary drift.
4. Update [design-notes/scorecard.md](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/design-notes/scorecard.md) if the pass materially changes integration strength.
5. Update [design-notes/improvement-queue.md](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/design-notes/improvement-queue.md) so completed duplication targets are removed.

After all passes:

1. Run targeted simulation or stress-test updates where the moved rules affect live scenarios.
2. Run one whole-draft cross-reference audit.
3. Add a brief de-duplication log entry to the scorecard or simulation findings.

## Execution Notes

- Do not try to solve drafting-quality and overlap-quality at the same time if that creates churn.
- Prefer short replacement clauses over silent deletion.
- If a moved rule still reads more like statute than constitutional principle, that is a second-pass compression issue, not a reason to leave duplication in place.
