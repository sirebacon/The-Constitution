# Preamble Design Notes

These notes explain the drafting choices in [preamble.md](/Users/chris/Documents/GitHub/The-Constitution/preamble.md). They are kept here so the preamble file itself remains only constitutional text.

---

## Equal Inclusion

**On "We the People ... all of us without exception"**

The original "We the People" excluded enslaved persons, women, and those without property. The revised opening corrects that directly and without ambiguity. It states equal inclusion before any right or structure is defined.

## Historical Honesty

**On acknowledging the broken promise**

Several modern constitutions, South Africa's most notably, open with an honest acknowledgment of historical injustice. This is not self-flagellation. It explains why a new constitutional settlement is necessary and sets the standard against which it should be judged.

## Limited but Capable Government

**On "strong enough to protect, limited enough to remain free"**

This remains the central tension in constitutional design. A government too weak cannot protect rights; a government too powerful becomes the threat to rights. The phrase "in every exercise of public power" makes the rule of law sound foundational rather than ornamental.

## Future Generations

**On future generations**

Design Principle 6, long-term thinking, belongs in the preamble rather than only in technical provisions. The duty to future generations, who cannot vote, petition, or lobby, is stated here as a founding commitment. "A planet capable of sustaining life" is concrete enough to ground Article XIII and the long-term fiscal provisions of Article II without sounding programmatic.

## Democratic Self-Defense

**On defending democracy in its own name**

This is the animating purpose of Article VI and the unamendable core of Article VIII, stated in plain language. The current phrasing ties democratic self-defense to law and equal citizenship, which makes it harder to misread as a free-floating warrant for partisan suppression.

## Brevity

**On brevity**

The preamble is five sentences. It is meant to be memorized, recited, and internalized, not studied as a procedural text. The articles provide the machinery; the preamble provides the reason.
