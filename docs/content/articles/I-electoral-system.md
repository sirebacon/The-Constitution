# Article I — The Electoral System

**Status:** `[ DRAFT ]`

---

## Scope

Establishes the right to vote, the mechanics of all federal elections, the rules for political party participation, and the citizens' initiative process. Election administration is delegated to the Electoral Commission under Article XII.

---

## Section 1 — The Right to Vote

**1.1** Every citizen of the United States who is eighteen years of age or older has the right to vote in all federal elections. This right shall not be denied, abridged, or conditioned on any basis including race, color, sex, gender, sexual orientation, religion, national origin, disability, income, property, or prior criminal conviction for which a sentence has been completed.

**1.2** The right to vote is a fundamental constitutional right. Any law, regulation, order, or practice that burdens the exercise of this right is subject to strict scrutiny by the federal courts.

**1.3** Every citizen entitled to vote has an equal right to have their vote counted. No electoral system, district boundary, or administrative procedure may be designed or administered with the purpose or effect of diluting the voting power of any group of citizens.

**1.4** Voting in federal elections is a right, not an obligation. No penalty may be imposed for failure to vote.

---

## Section 2 — Voter Registration

**2.1** Every citizen is automatically registered to vote upon turning eighteen years of age, or upon becoming a citizen, using information held by federal and state agencies. No citizen is required to take affirmative action to register. For persons naturalizing as adults, registration shall be completed through the naturalization process itself — the Electoral Commission shall coordinate with the agency responsible for administering naturalization to ensure that every new citizen is registered in their state of domicile at the time their naturalization is certified, without any separate application required.

**2.2** Citizens may update their registration at any time, including on the day of an election.

**2.3** A citizen's registration may be removed only upon their death, upon their written request, or upon a judicial finding of ineligibility. Removal on any other basis is unlawful.

**2.4** The Electoral Commission (Article XII) shall maintain the national voter registration system and publish the full register, redacted for personal security information, as a public record.

---

## Section 3 — Federal Election Schedule

**3.1** Federal elections shall be held on the first Tuesday of November in even-numbered years. This schedule may not be altered by executive order or declaration of emergency. Congress may by law move the election date only in the event of a catastrophic national emergency, and only by a 2/3 vote of both chambers, and only to a date within 60 days of the original date.

**3.2** All federal elections shall be conducted over a period of no less than three days, ending on the Tuesday specified in Section 3.1. Early voting shall be available for no less than fifteen days before the election. For purposes of Section 3.1, a "catastrophic national emergency" that may justify congressional action to move the election date is limited to: (i) armed conflict occurring on United States soil; (ii) a pandemic causing the death of at least 10,000 persons per week nationally as certified by the Director of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention; or (iii) a natural disaster that has destroyed critical infrastructure in three or more states as certified by the Federal Emergency Management Agency. No single criterion is independently sufficient — an electoral delay requires a concurrent affirmative finding by all three branches: a presidential declaration, a 2/3 vote of both chambers of Congress, and a written determination by a majority of the Supreme Court that the emergency renders the conduct of a free and fair election on the scheduled date impossible.

**3.3** Election Day shall be a federal public holiday.

**3.4** No federal official may declare a national emergency within 60 days of a scheduled federal election in a manner that restricts access to polling places, reduces early voting, or delays certification of results, unless a federal court finds the emergency poses a direct, imminent, and unavoidable threat to public safety that cannot be mitigated by other means. Any such court proceeding shall be subject to strict scrutiny. Standing to challenge an emergency declaration under this Section is held by any registered voter and by the Electoral Commission. Courts shall resolve such challenges on an expedited basis, with a final ruling issued within 72 hours of the filing of a complaint.

---

## Section 4 — Elections to the House of Representatives

### 4.1 — Method: Single Transferable Vote

Elections to the House of Representatives shall use the Single Transferable Vote method. Voters shall rank candidates in order of preference. Seats shall be allocated by the Droop quota formula. Voters shall be permitted to rank a number of candidates not less than the number of seats in their district. The Electoral Commission shall set the maximum number of rankings by regulation, calibrated to ballot usability. A ballot that ranks fewer than the maximum number of candidates remains valid and shall not be treated as exhausted until all ranked candidates have been eliminated or elected.

### 4.2 — Multi-Member Districts

The nation shall be divided into congressional districts. Each district shall return between three and seven members to the House of Representatives. The number of members returned by each district shall be determined by the Independent Redistricting Commission (Section 8) in proportion to the district's population, subject to the three-member minimum.

### 4.3 — Terms

Members of the House of Representatives shall serve four-year terms. Districts shall be divided by the Independent Redistricting Commission into two equal groups. Group A districts shall hold elections in years divisible by four; Group B districts shall hold elections in years producing a remainder of two when divided by four. This staggering ensures that approximately half of all House seats are contested every two years.

### 4.4 — Vacancies

A vacancy in the House of Representatives shall be filled by a special election held within 90 days of the vacancy arising. If fewer than 6 months remain in the term, the seat may remain vacant until the next scheduled general election. The Electoral Commission shall complete certification of any special election within 45 days of the election being held, regardless of any pending disputes. If the Commission fails to certify within that period, certification shall proceed automatically under court supervision, with the United States District Court for the District of Columbia designated to appoint a special master to complete certification within 15 additional days.

---

## Section 5 — Elections to the Regional Assembly

### 5.1 — Method: Ranked Choice Voting

Elections to the Regional Assembly shall use Ranked Choice Voting (Instant Runoff). Each state shall elect its Regional Assembly members in statewide elections. Voters shall rank candidates in order of preference. The candidate with the fewest first-preference votes is eliminated in each round and their votes redistributed until a candidate receives a majority.

### 5.2 — Staggered Terms

Members of the Regional Assembly shall serve six-year terms. Regional Assembly seats within each region shall be divided into three equal classes. One class within each region shall face election every two years, ensuring continuity of representation.

### 5.3 — Vacancies

A vacancy in the Regional Assembly shall be filled by special election held within 120 days of the vacancy arising. If fewer than 12 months remain in the term, the Governor of the relevant state shall appoint a temporary replacement who shall serve until the next scheduled general election, at which point a special election shall fill the remainder of the term. The Electoral Commission shall complete certification of any special election within 45 days of the election being held, regardless of any pending disputes. If the Commission fails to certify within that period, certification shall proceed automatically under court supervision, with the United States District Court for the District of Columbia designated to appoint a special master to complete certification within 15 additional days.

---

## Section 6 — Presidential Election

**6.1** The President shall be elected by direct national popular vote using Ranked Choice Voting. The candidate who achieves a majority of votes after the elimination of lower-ranked candidates shall be elected President.

**6.2** The Electoral College is abolished. No intermediate body shall stand between the national popular vote and the election of the President.

**6.3** The Electoral Commission shall certify the result of the presidential election no later than 30 days after Election Day. Certification may be delayed only by order of a federal court with jurisdiction over an active election dispute. Litigation filed primarily to delay certification rather than to vindicate a genuine legal claim is frivolous and subjects the filing party and their counsel to sanctions under the applicable rules of federal civil procedure and any additional sanctions established by the Electoral Commission by regulation. If no federal court has issued a stay of certification by day 30, certification proceeds automatically without further action by any officer or body.

**6.4** Presidential and Vice Presidential candidates shall run jointly on a single ticket. The Vice President is elected as part of the same ticket as the President and has no separate electoral mandate.

---

## Section 7 — Apportionment

**7.1** A census of the population shall be conducted every ten years, in years ending in zero, by an agency independent of partisan direction. The census shall count all persons residing in the United States regardless of citizenship status.

**7.2** Following each census, the 500 seats of the House of Representatives shall be reapportioned among the states in proportion to their populations, with each state guaranteed a minimum of one seat.

**7.3** Following each census, Regional Assembly seats within each region shall be reapportioned among the states of that region in proportion to their populations, with each state guaranteed a minimum of one seat in the Regional Assembly.

**7.4** Reapportionment shall take effect for the first election held more than one year after the census is completed.

---

## Section 8 — Independent Redistricting

**8.1** Congressional district boundaries for House elections shall be drawn by the Independent Redistricting Commission, an independent body within the Electoral Commission (Article XII).

**8.2** Districts shall be drawn according to the following criteria, in order of priority:
  (a) Equal population, within a deviation of no more than 1%
  (b) Contiguity — all parts of a district must be connected
  (c) Compliance with the Voting Rights provisions of Article V
  (d) Preservation of existing communities of interest, including municipal and county boundaries where possible
  (e) Geographic compactness
  (f) Partisan symmetry — the map as a whole shall not produce a systematic electoral advantage for any political party disproportionate to that party's statewide vote share. The Electoral Commission shall establish by regulation the methods and metrics used to measure partisan symmetry, which shall include at minimum the efficiency gap and mean-median difference. A map that produces a projected partisan bias exceeding a threshold established by the Commission shall not be adopted without written findings explaining why no compliant alternative exists. This Constitution establishes the following floors that the Commission may tighten but may not loosen: the efficiency gap for any adopted map may not exceed 7%; the mean-median difference may not exceed 5%. These are maximum permissible values — the Commission retains authority to adopt stricter standards by regulation.
  (g) No criterion may consider the political affiliation, voting history, or place of residence of any individual voter for the purpose of favoring or disfavoring any party. Partisan symmetry under Section 8.2(f) is measured as an output constraint on the map — it does not permit drawing lines with partisan intent.

**8.3** District maps shall be drawn using open, publicly auditable algorithmic methods. All data, algorithms, and intermediate outputs used in drawing maps shall be published before the maps take effect.

**8.4** Proposed maps shall be subject to a 60-day public comment period. The Commission shall publish a written response to all substantive comments received.

**8.5** Final maps may be challenged in federal court within 30 days of adoption. Courts shall apply the criteria of Section 8.2 in reviewing any challenge. A court finding a map non-compliant shall order the Commission to produce a remedial map within 60 days.

**8.6 — Deadlock resolution.** If the Commission cannot adopt a final map by majority vote within 30 days of the close of the public comment period, or cannot adopt a court-ordered remedial map within the required period, the following process applies:

  (a) Each faction of the Commission that has submitted a competing map proposal shall certify its proposal to the Chief Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit within 5 days
  (b) The Chief Judge shall convene a panel of three federal circuit judges, selected at random from circuits other than the D.C. Circuit, to serve as the redistricting panel
  (c) The panel shall select one of the submitted proposals — without modification — within 30 days, applying the criteria of Section 8.2 as its sole standard
  (d) The panel may not draw its own map; it may only select among the proposals submitted by the Commission
  (e) The panel's selection is final and not subject to appeal except on the grounds that the selected map violates the criteria of Section 8.2
  (f) If all proposals submitted to the panel fail to meet the constitutional criteria of Section 8.2, the panel shall not select a non-compliant map without first issuing written notice to each Commission faction identifying the deficiencies and requesting revised proposals within 15 days. If the revised proposals remain non-compliant, the panel shall select the least non-compliant proposal and shall simultaneously certify the map to the United States District Court for the District of Columbia for immediate judicial review. The selected map takes provisional effect pending that review, but the court shall issue a final ruling within 60 days and may order further remediation.

## Section 9 — Party Qualification and Representation Threshold

**9.1** Any political party that obtains signatures of at least 0.1% of registered voters in at least 20 states within a 12-month period shall be a qualified party entitled to appear on federal election ballots.

**9.2** A party that wins at least 4% of the total national vote cast in elections to the House of Representatives shall be entitled to have its candidates considered for seat allocation under the STV formula in those elections. For purposes of this threshold, parties that are legally distinct but functionally unified shall be aggregated: two or more parties whose votes would otherwise be counted separately shall be treated as a single party if, in the most recent comparable federal election cycle, they shared a common organizational structure, shared top officers (including the same chairperson or executive director), or ran the same individual as a candidate in more than 30% of contested House districts. The Electoral Commission shall make aggregation determinations before each election, with findings subject to expedited judicial review.

**9.3** The threshold in Section 9.2 does not apply to individual candidates running without party affiliation. Independent candidates may win seats through the STV process regardless of national vote totals.

**9.4** No law may prohibit the formation or operation of a political party on the basis of its ideology, platform, or political positions, except where the party's stated purpose is the violent overthrow of this Constitution or the permanent removal of the right to vote.

**9.5** A qualified party, and any independent or party candidate lawfully entitled to appear on a federal ballot, is entitled under neutral and publicly stated rules to nondiscriminatory access to core public or publicly regulated election infrastructure necessary for meaningful federal contestation. Congress may by law authorize the Electoral Commission to define and enforce such access with respect to official ballot information systems, publicly funded or officially designated candidate debates, public voter-information feeds, and other election-related channels whose exclusionary control would materially degrade effective political competition. This Section does not require private persons to endorse, sponsor, or carry partisan viewpoints except where Article VI permits narrowly tailored continuity or nondiscrimination duties for dominant civic intermediaries.

## Section 10 — Election Administration

**10.1** All federal elections shall be administered by the Electoral Commission established under Article XII. No federal or state official subject to election may direct, supervise, or exercise authority over the administration of any election in which they are a candidate.

**10.2** The Electoral Commission shall establish uniform minimum standards for federal elections, including ballot design, voting equipment, accessibility for voters with disabilities, and procedures for counting and certifying results. States may exceed but not fall below these standards.

**10.3** All voting systems used in federal elections must produce a paper record verifiable by the voter at the time of voting. No voting system that does not produce such a record may be used in a federal election.

**10.4** Results shall be certified by the Electoral Commission within 30 days of Election Day. Certification may be delayed only by order of a federal court. No elected or appointed official may refuse to certify, delay certification without court order, or substitute their judgment for the certified result.

**10.5** Attempts to interfere with the administration of an election, intimidate voters or election workers, or obstruct the counting or certification of votes are federal crimes and constitute grounds for the anti-subversion penalties of Article VI.

**10.6** The Electoral Commission may issue expedited temporary orders, subject to prompt judicial review, to prevent or remedy discriminatory exclusion of qualified parties or lawful candidates from the election infrastructure described in Section 9.5 where delay would materially impair a pending federal election.

## Section 11 — Citizens' Initiative

**11.1** Citizens may initiate a national referendum on the repeal or affirmation of any existing federal law by submitting a petition signed by at least 3% of registered voters, with signatures gathered from at least 30 states.

**11.2** A petition meeting the requirements of Section 11.1 shall trigger a national referendum administered by the Electoral Commission, held at the next scheduled federal election or within 12 months, whichever is sooner.

**11.3** A referendum under this Section passes upon approval by a majority of votes cast, provided that at least 40% of registered voters participate. Deliberate coordinated abstention organized for the purpose of defeating the participation floor — rather than genuine non-participation — is itself a violation of democratic integrity. The Electoral Commission may investigate credible evidence of coordinated abstention campaigns and, upon a finding of probable cause, refer the matter to the Accountability Commission for appropriate action. Evidence sufficient for a referral includes public statements, organizational communications, or patterns of conduct demonstrating that the abstention was organized as a strategic tactic to cause the referendum to fail the participation threshold rather than to express genuine non-participation.

**11.4** A law repealed by referendum may not be re-enacted in substantially the same form for four years without another referendum.

**11.5** The Citizens' Initiative may not be used to:
  (a) Reduce, restrict, or repeal any right enumerated in Article V
  (b) Target any specific individual, group, or minority for differential treatment
  (c) Override a judicial ruling on constitutional grounds
  (d) Amend this Constitution (which is governed by Article XI)

**11.6** The Supreme Court shall have jurisdiction to review any proposed referendum for compliance with Section 11.5 before it is put to a vote.

## Section 12 — Overseas Citizens

**12.1 — Constitutional basis.** The constitutional principles governing overseas citizenship, domicile assignment, and continued federal political membership are established in Article IX. This Section governs the administration of overseas federal voting.

**12.2 — Electoral assignment.** Congress and the Electoral Commission shall assign overseas citizens to the proper federal electoral home consistent with Article IX and shall prevent duplicate registration or multiple-state participation in the same election.

**12.3 — Registration.** Overseas citizens shall register with the Electoral Commission through a secure federal overseas voter registration system. The Electoral Commission shall administer overseas balloting, maintain overseas registration rolls, and publish guidance sufficient to make overseas federal voting practical and reviewable.

**12.4 — Ballot delivery and return.** The Electoral Commission shall provide reliable ballot-delivery and ballot-return methods, including electronic and postal options where lawful and secure, sufficient to allow overseas citizens to cast timely ballots in federal elections.

**12.5 — Counting rule.** Overseas ballots received within 30 days after election day shall be counted, provided the ballot was cast on or before election day.

## Section 13 — Recall of Elected Federal Officials

**13.1 — Purpose.** Impeachment and chamber expulsion are the primary mechanisms for removing federal officials for misconduct. This Section provides a separate, constituent-initiated recall mechanism for elected members of Congress and the Regional Assembly where those mechanisms have failed or where the ground for removal is loss of constituent confidence rather than formal misconduct. The presidential recall is governed by Article III, Section 14.

**13.2 — Scope.** The recall mechanism established in this Section applies to:
  (a) Members of the House of Representatives, recallable by the registered voters of their district
  (b) Members of the Regional Assembly, recallable by the registered voters of their state

Federal judges and appointed officials are not subject to recall. Judicial independence requires insulation from popular pressure; the remedy for judicial misconduct is the process established in Article IV.

**13.3 — Petition requirements.** A recall election is triggered when a petition is submitted to the Electoral Commission meeting all of the following requirements:

  (a) **Signatures**: The petition must be signed by registered voters equal to at least 20% of the total votes cast in the official's most recent election, gathered from within the official's district or state
  (b) **Collection period**: Signatures must be gathered within 120 consecutive days
  (c) **Verification**: The Electoral Commission shall verify signatures within 30 days of submission and certify whether the threshold has been met
  (d) **Timing restriction**: A petition may not be filed during the first 12 months of the official's term, or within 6 months of the next scheduled general election for that seat
  (e) **One attempt per term**: Only one recall petition against the same official may be filed in any single term of office. A petition that fails to reach the signature threshold, or that results in a recall election in which the official survives, may not be refiled during the same term.

**13.4 — Recall election.** Upon certification of a valid petition, the Electoral Commission shall conduct a recall election within 60 days.

  (a) The ballot shall present a single question: whether the official should be removed from office
  (b) Removal requires a majority of votes cast, provided that total turnout equals at least 40% of registered voters in the district or state. If the turnout threshold is not met, the recall fails regardless of the vote distribution. Deliberate coordinated abstention organized for the purpose of defeating the participation floor is itself a violation of democratic integrity; the Electoral Commission may investigate and refer credible evidence of such conduct to the Accountability Commission, applying the same standard as Section 11.3
  (c) The official subject to recall may campaign against removal and shall not be suspended from their duties pending the outcome

**13.5 — Vacancy following recall.** If an official is removed by recall:

  (a) For a **House member**: a special election shall be held in the district within 90 days, conducted by Single Transferable Vote among newly nominated candidates. The original STV count from the prior general election is not re-used. The winner of the special election serves the remainder of the recalled member's original term, preserving the district's stagger class. A special election does not reset the term clock or alter the district's scheduled general election date.
  (b) For a **Regional Assembly member**: a special election shall be held statewide within 90 days, conducted by Ranked Choice Voting among newly nominated candidates
  (c) If fewer than 6 months remain in the term at the time of removal, the seat shall remain vacant until the next scheduled general election

**13.6 — No recall for protected conduct.** A recall petition may not cite as its basis any vote cast, speech made, or position taken by the official in the exercise of their legislative duties. The recall mechanism is a check on the official's conduct and fitness for office, not a mechanism for overriding legislative votes the petitioners disagree with. The Electoral Commission shall reject any petition whose stated basis primarily consists of the official's legislative record. Where a petition combines protected legislative conduct with non-legislative allegations, the Electoral Commission may look through pretextual non-legislative allegations — that is, allegations included solely to avoid the bar rather than reflecting a genuine independent ground — and shall reject the petition if, stripping away the pretextual allegations, the dominant and operative basis remains protected legislative conduct.

**13.7 — Administration.** The Electoral Commission shall administer all recall petitions and recall elections under this Section. It shall publish regulations governing petition form, signature verification, and recall election procedures.
