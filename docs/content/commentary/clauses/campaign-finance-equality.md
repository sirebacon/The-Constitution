# Clause Note: Campaign Finance And Political Equality

## Why Money In Politics Is A Constitutional Problem

If large concentrations of wealth can dominate political communication, candidate viability, and access to representation, then formal political equality becomes increasingly fictional.

That is why this draft treats campaign finance as part of the democratic order rather than a peripheral regulatory field.

## Why The Draft Does Not Freeze One Policy Model

The Constitution should not try to encode every reporting threshold, public-financing ratio, or disclosure form.

What it can do is establish that political equality, transparency, and anti-corruption are constitutional values strong enough to justify meaningful regulation of political money.

## Why This Matters For Democracy

The point is not to abolish political disagreement or political advocacy.

It is to keep representative government from drifting into a system where access to office and influence over public power depend too heavily on private wealth.
