# Clause Note: Naturalized Citizens And The Presidency

## Why Reject The Natural-Born Requirement?

The natural-born-citizen rule in the current U.S. Constitution creates a caste distinction inside citizenship.

This draft rejects that distinction because equal citizenship should mean that citizenship, not ancestry or birthplace, defines full political membership.

## Why This Is Democracy-Protective

Excluding naturalized citizens from the presidency sends the signal that some citizens permanently belong on a lower constitutional tier.

That is hard to reconcile with a constitution built around equal membership, equal dignity, and universal political belonging.

## Why This Does Not Ignore Security Concerns

The draft does not deny that some positions can involve legitimate security screening.

It handles that concern through narrowly reviewable sensitive-office rules and general law, not through a blanket hereditary-style exclusion from the highest office.
