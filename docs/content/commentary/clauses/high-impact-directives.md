# Clause Note: Fast-Track Review For High-Impact Directives

## Why This Clause Exists

Presidents can change the practical legal landscape very quickly through orders or directives that take effect before normal litigation can catch up.

That creates a familiar problem: even if the directive is later struck down, the damage may already be done.

## Why The Fast-Track Is Narrow

The draft does not create accelerated review for every executive order.

It is limited to directives that are unusually consequential because they:

- claim emergency, inherent, or unilateral authority
- materially alter rights or duties on a nationwide basis
- or affect especially sensitive domains like elections, civil liberties, immigration status, tariffs, or military force

The point is to prevent timing from becoming a source of unconstitutional power without turning the judiciary into a daily supervisor of executive housekeeping.

## Why Ordinary Review Was Not Enough

In constitutional crises, delay can function like permission.

This clause exists because some presidential actions are so sweeping that a slow path to review is not a neutral baseline.
