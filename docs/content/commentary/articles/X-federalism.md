# Article X Notes

## What This Article Is Trying To Fix

Federalism can protect pluralism and local self-government, but it can also become a shield for democratic erosion, rights denial, and opportunistic conflict between levels of government.

This article tries to preserve federalism without romanticizing it.

## Why The Article Uses A Democratic Floor

The draft does not treat state autonomy as absolute.

States remain constitutionally important, but only inside a system that preserves republican and democratic government, equal citizenship, and federal constitutional supremacy. Federalism is protected, but not as a license for anti-democratic or anti-rights enclaves.

## Why Local Self-Government Also Matters

The article is not only about national power versus states.

It also recognizes that states can become their own centralizers. That is why the draft includes limits on arbitrary state preemption of local democratic choices where no sufficient public interest justifies it.

## Why This Article Needs Scenario Testing

Federalism disputes often look narrow until they become structural.

That is why the recent simulation work focused on preemption overreach, democratic-floor enforcement, and digital-border issues. The goal is to make sure the article can handle modern intergovernmental conflict, not just older textbook federalism disputes.
