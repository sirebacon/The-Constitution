# Article XI Notes

## Why This Draft Uses An Unamendable Core

A democratic constitution should be amendable, but it should not be amendable into non-democracy.

The unamendable core is meant to protect the minimum conditions that make lawful politics possible at all: suffrage, elections, transfer of power, equal citizenship, judicial independence, separation of powers, and related foundations.

## Why The Core Is Narrow

The core is not a list of everything good.

It is intentionally narrower than the full set of constitutional values. Social rights, fiscal design, institutional details, and many other important parts of the draft remain amendable because they are not the minimum conditions of democratic legitimacy in the same way.

## Why The Article Bars Functional Evasion

Authoritarian amendments often preserve constitutional language while destroying constitutional reality.

That is why the article blocks not only literal repeal of the democratic core, but also amendments that functionally destroy free elections, lawful alternation in power, or effective checks on concentrated power.

## Why This Matters For The Whole Draft

Without this article, an otherwise democratic constitution can become a legally self-liquidating system.

The amendment article is what makes the rest of the anti-authoritarian architecture durable over time.
