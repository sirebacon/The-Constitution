# Article VIII Notes

## Why Government Ethics Needs Constitutional Weight

Corruption does not only distort policy. It corrodes democratic legitimacy and makes every other constitutional safeguard harder to trust.

This article gives ethics rules constitutional importance because corruption is not incidental to constitutional failure. It is often one of its main engines.

## What The Article Is Designed Against

The article targets:

- self-dealing
- office-for-private-gain behavior
- hidden financial exposure
- contractor capture
- regulatory capture
- political protection for systemically powerful financial actors

## Why This Article Sits Beside Article XIII

Article VIII owns substantive ethics, corruption, and integrity duties.

Article XIII owns the broader framework for federal agencies and administrative structure.

That boundary matters because without it, the draft would either duplicate rules or let ethics become diffuse and unenforceable.

## Why The Article Avoids Becoming A Full Compliance Code

Ethics provisions can easily become too technical.

The constitutional task is to establish the duty of public office, anti-capture principles, and a floor of transparency and enforceability. Detailed reporting systems, audit practice, and regulatory administration should remain mostly in law unless they are needed to stop obvious evasion.
