# How A Bill Becomes Law

This visual guide shows the legislative path in this constitution, including the ordinary route, what happens after a veto, and how the system resolves deadlock between the House and the Regional Assembly.

## How To Read This Guide

The guide has three paths:

- ordinary passage to law
- veto and override
- deadlock and conference resolution

Use the path buttons to focus on one route through the legislative process at a time.

## What This Guide Covers

- bicameral passage in identical form
- conference committees and reconciled bills
- the 15-day presidential action window
- no pocket veto
- 2/3 override after veto
- House-initiated floor-vote request and 60-day conference backstop
- pre-enactment single-subject challenge

## What This Guide Does Not Do

This guide is an orientation layer, not the constitutional text itself.

For exact wording and legal detail, read:

- [Article II](articles/II-legislature.md)
- [Article III](articles/III-executive.md)
