# How Officials Are Removed

This visual guide shows every path for removing a federal official under this constitution — who triggers it, who decides, what threshold applies, and what happens after.

## How To Read This Guide

Each table covers one officer type. Use the category buttons to focus on the President, Congress, judges, or constitutional organs. Each row is one distinct removal path.

## What This Guide Covers

- three paths for removing the President: impeachment, ACC prosecution, and recall
- expulsion and recall for members of Congress
- impeachment and discipline for federal judges
- removal of Electoral Commission and Accountability Commission members

## What This Guide Does Not Do

This guide is an orientation layer, not the constitutional text itself.

For exact wording and legal detail, read:

- [Article I](articles/I-electoral-system.md) — recall for Congress
- [Article II](articles/II-legislature.md) — congressional expulsion
- [Article III](articles/III-executive.md) — presidential removal
- [Article IV](articles/IV-judiciary.md) — judicial removal
