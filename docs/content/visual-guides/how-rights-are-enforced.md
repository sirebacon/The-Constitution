# How Rights Are Enforced

This visual guide explains how rights claims move through the constitutional system: who can challenge unlawful action, when courts must move quickly, and what rights remain protected even in emergencies.

## How To Read This Guide

The guide has three paths:

- ordinary enforcement
- expedited review
- emergency rights protection

Use the path buttons to focus on one enforcement route at a time.

## What This Guide Covers

- concrete-injury rights challenges in federal court
- judicial review of laws and executive action
- fast-track review for time-sensitive constitutional disputes
- anti-delay backstops when higher courts stall
- non-derogable rights in emergencies
- 72-hour challenge rule for rights suspensions

## What This Guide Does Not Do

This guide is an orientation layer, not the constitutional text itself.

For exact wording and legal detail, read:

- [Article IV](articles/IV-judiciary.md)
- [Article V](articles/V-rights.md)
- [Article III](articles/III-executive.md)
